# Autonomous Integrity Assessment

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework used to continuously assess ORYNTH architecture integrity through autonomous evaluation of structure, relationships, governance conditions, and reference alignment.

---

## Integrity Assessment Principle

Autonomous integrity assessment evaluates whether architecture states remain consistent with defined constraints, provenance requirements, governance conditions, and continuity expectations.

Integrity is preserved through continuous verification.

---

## Assessment Domains

Includes:

- architecture coherence;
- semantic integrity;
- governance alignment;
- dependency consistency;
- provenance verification;
- transition validity;
- reference preservation;
- continuity assessment.

---

↓

## Integrity Constraints

Autonomous integrity assessment preserves:

- architecture identity;
- semantic boundaries;
- provenance;
- uncertainty representation;
- governance requirements;
- validation evidence;
- reference continuity.

---

## Principle

Autonomous Integrity Assessment enables ORYNTH to continuously evaluate architectural health while preserving coherence, traceability, and controlled evolution.
