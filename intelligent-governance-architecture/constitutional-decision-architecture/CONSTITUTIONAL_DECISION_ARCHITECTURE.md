# Constitutional Decision Architecture

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework used to structure constitutional decision processes, authority relationships, decision constraints, and governance continuity across the ORYNTH ecosystem.

---

## Decision Principle

Constitutional decision architecture ensures decisions are evaluated through defined principles, preserved authority boundaries, contextual conditions, and validated transition pathways.

---

## Decision Domains

Includes:

- constitutional principles;
- authority relationships;
- decision eligibility;
- constraint evaluation;
- validation mechanisms;
- continuity preservation.

---

## Decision Lifecycle


IDENTIFY DECISION CONTEXT

↓

ASSESS CONSTITUTIONAL CONDITIONS

↓

VERIFY AUTHORITY AND CONSTRAINTS

↓

EVALUATE DECISION ELIGIBILITY

↓

EXECUTE GOVERNED TRANSITION

↓

PRESERVE DECISION HISTORY


---

## Principle

A constitutional decision architecture provides a structured foundation for maintaining decision integrity across evolving ORYNTH systems.
