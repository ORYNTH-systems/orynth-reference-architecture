# Governance Model

Status:

INITIALIZATION

Classification:

Architectural Integration Layer

---

## Purpose

Define governance relationships for the Morning Star Phase II Architecture Integration Layer while preserving constitutional authority established during Phase I.

---

## Governance Principle

Architecture organizes capability relationships.

Governance preserves authority boundaries.

## Governance Responsibilities

Phase II Governance governs:

- integration permissions;
- capability relationships;
- architectural boundaries;
- runtime authority;
- verification requirements;
- transition controls.

---

## Authority Preservation

Phase II shall preserve:

- originating constitutional authority;
- capability ownership;
- provenance chains;
- verification requirements;
- reconstruction requirements.

---

## Governance Constraints

The Architecture Integration Layer shall not:

- redefine Phase I capabilities;
- transfer authority;
- remove constitutional constraints;
- bypass verification;
- create unsupported interpretations.

---

## Decision Model

Architectural decisions require:

1. Defined scope.
2. Identified authority.
3. Preserved provenance.
4. Verification pathway.
5. Reconstruction capability.

---

---

## Governance Invariant

Integration may coordinate authority.

Integration may not create authority.

---

## Success Condition

Governance succeeds when architectural expansion occurs without constitutional drift.
