# Governance Execution

Status:

INITIALIZATION

Classification:

Phase II Architecture Integration Layer

---

## Purpose

Define the governance execution mechanism responsible for preserving constitutional authority while coordinating integrated architecture operations.

---

## Governance Principle

Governance controls admissible action.

Governance does not replace the capabilities it governs.

---

## Governance Position
Constitutional Authority

    ↓

Capability Authority

    ↓

Architecture Governance

    ↓

Runtime Execution

    ↓

Verification


---

## Governance Responsibilities

The Governance Execution Layer manages:

- authorization pathways;
- architectural decisions;
- capability permissions;
- execution eligibility;
- transition review;
- verification requirements.

---

## Governance Execution Sequence


REQUEST

↓

DECISION

EXECUTION AUTHORIZATION

↓

VERIFICATION

↓

RECORDING


---

## Governance Requirements

Every governed action requires:

- identified authority source;
- defined scope;
- preserved provenance;
- explicit constraints;
- verification pathway.

---

## Governance Failure Conditions

Governance execution fails when:

- authority cannot be identified;
- decision ownership is ambiguous;
- constraints are bypassed;
- evidence cannot be produced;
- reconstruction becomes impossible.

---

## Governance Invariant

Governance preserves the conditions under which architecture remains trustworthy.

---

## Success Condition

Governance execution succeeds when integrated operations remain authorized, traceable, and constitutionally aligned.
