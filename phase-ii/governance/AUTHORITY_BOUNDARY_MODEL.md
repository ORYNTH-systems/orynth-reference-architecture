# Authority Boundary Model

Status:

INITIALIZATION

Classification:

Phase II Architecture Integration Layer

---

## Purpose

Define authority boundaries between constitutional capabilities, architecture integration, runtime execution, and applications.

---

## Authority Principle

Authority must remain explicit across all architectural layers.

---

## Authority Layers


Layer 1

Constitutional Authority

    ↓

Layer 2

Capability Authority

    ↓

Layer 3

Architecture Authority

    ↓

Layer 4

Runtime Authority

    ↓

Layer 5

Application Authority


---

## Layer Definitions

## Constitutional Authority

Defines:

- foundational rules;
- identity;
- invariants;
- governance ownership.

---

## Capability Authority

Defines:

- capability purpose;
- permitted operations;
- capability boundaries.

---

## Architecture Authority

Defines:

- composition;
- relationships;
- integration rules.

---

## Runtime Authority

Defines:

- execution order;
- operational coordination;
- state management.

---

## Application Authority

Defines:

- usage;
- interaction;
- deployment context.

---

## Boundary Constraints

Lower layers may:

- execute;
- coordinate;
- apply.

Lower layers may not:

- redefine;
- supersede;
- modify higher authority.

---

## Authority Violation Conditions

A violation occurs when:

- runtime changes constitutional meaning;
- applications bypass governance;
- integration creates unauthorized capability;
- execution modifies identity.

---

## Authority Invariant

Coordination does not equal ownership.

Execution does not equal authority creation.
