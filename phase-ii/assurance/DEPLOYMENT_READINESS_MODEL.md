# Deployment Readiness Model

Status:

INITIALIZATION

Classification:

Phase II Assurance Hardening Layer

---

## Purpose

Define readiness conditions required before operational deployment of integrated Morning Star architecture.

---

## Readiness Principle

Deployment requires demonstrated integrity, not only completed implementation.

---

## Readiness Dimensions

### Architectural Readiness

Confirms:

- architecture completeness;
- component relationships;
- interface stability.

---

### Runtime Readiness

Confirms:

- execution reliability;
- state management;
- lifecycle behavior.

---

### Governance Readiness

Confirms:

- authority resolution;
- decision pathways;
- constraint enforcement.

---

### Assurance Readiness

Confirms:

- evidence availability;
- validation coverage;
- reconstruction capability.

---

## Failure Conditions

Deployment readiness fails when:

- required evidence is unavailable;
- runtime behavior is unverified;
- authority boundaries are unclear;
- recovery capability is insufficient.

---

## Readiness Invariant

Deployment extends a verified architecture; it does not establish one.
