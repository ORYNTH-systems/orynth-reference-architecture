# Reconstruction Validation

Status:

INITIALIZATION

Classification:

Phase II Architecture Integration Layer

---

## Purpose

Define validation requirements proving integrated architecture states can be reconstructed from preserved evidence.

---

## Reconstruction Principle

A valid architecture state must remain reproducible after execution.

---

## Reconstruction Model


Recorded Inputs

Authority Context

Execution History

State Transitions

Verification Evidence

=

Reconstructed State


---

## Reconstruction Requirements

A reconstruction process requires:

- original context;
- capability configuration;
- authority relationships;
- execution sequence;
- resulting state;
- verification outcome.

---

## Validation Sequence

Step 01

Load recorded architectural state.

---

Step 02

Restore execution context.

---

Step 03

Restore capability relationships.

---

Step 04

Replay governed transitions.

---

Step 05

Compare reconstructed state.

---

Step 06

Validate equivalence.

---

## Reconstruction Equality

A reconstruction is valid when:

RecordedState

=

ReconstructedState

under equivalent constitutional conditions.

---

## Reconstruction Failure Conditions

Failure occurs when:

- evidence is incomplete;
- state history is unavailable;
- authority cannot be resolved;
- equivalent output cannot be reproduced.

---

## Reconstruction Invariant

A trustworthy architecture is not only executable.

It is explainable, reviewable, and reconstructable.

---

## Success Condition

Phase II reconstruction validation succeeds when integrated architecture states remain independently reproducible from preserved evidence.
