# Evolution State Modeling

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework for representing evolution states within ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Evolution Principle

Evolution represents the controlled transformation of an architecture while preserving its defining identity, purpose, and continuity.

An evolution state preserves:

- current architectural state;
- proposed transformation;
- governing conditions;
- transition pathway;
- resulting future state.

---

## Evolution Domains

Includes:

- state transition;
- adaptive change;
- capability development;
- structural refinement;
- future-state formation;
- architectural maturity.

---

## Evolution Lifecycle


CURRENT ARCHITECTURAL STATE

↓

EVOLUTION CONDITIONS IDENTIFIED

↓

TRANSFORMATION REQUIREMENTS ANALYZED

↓

EVOLUTION PATHWAY FORMED

↓

GOVERNED TRANSITION OCCURS

↓

FUTURE STATE ESTABLISHED


---

## Evolution Requirements

Evolution states must preserve:

- architectural identity;
- historical lineage;
- governance conditions;
- transformation rationale;
- validation evidence;
- continuity relationships.

---

## Principle

Evolution State Modeling ensures architectural transformation occurs through controlled progression rather than uncontrolled change.
