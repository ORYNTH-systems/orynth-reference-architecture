# Evolution Governance System

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the governance framework for maintaining integrity during ORYNTH / Morning Star architectural evolution.

---

## Evolution Governance Principle

Evolution enables transformation while preserving:

- architectural identity;
- constitutional purpose;
- semantic integrity;
- evidence lineage;
- continuity requirements.

Change alone does not constitute valid evolution. Evolution requires governed transition.

---

## Governance Domains

Includes:

- evolution eligibility;
- transformation review;
- identity preservation;
- dependency impact;
- validation requirements;
- future-state acceptance.

---

## Evolution Governance Lifecycle


EVOLUTION STATE IDENTIFIED

↓

TRANSFORMATION REVIEW INITIATED

↓

IMPACT CONDITIONS ANALYZED

↓

GOVERNANCE REQUIREMENTS APPLIED

↓

EVOLUTION TRANSITION APPROVED

↓

FUTURE STATE ACCEPTED


---

## Governance Requirements

Evolution governance must preserve:

- originating state;
- transformation rationale;
- affected relationships;
- authorization conditions;
- validation evidence;
- historical continuity.

---

## Principle

The Evolution Governance System ensures architectural transformation remains controlled, explainable, and aligned with long-term identity.
