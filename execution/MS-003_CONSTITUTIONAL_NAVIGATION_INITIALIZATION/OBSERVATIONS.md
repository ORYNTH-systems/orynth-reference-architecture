## Observation 1

A constitutional ecosystem cannot be entered arbitrarily.

Entry order affects comprehension, admissibility, and reconstruction.

---

## Observation 2

Morning Star exists to govern orientation rather than scientific discovery.

Its constitutional responsibility is entry into knowledge, not creation of new scientific knowledge.

---

## Observation 3

Frameworks possess prerequisite relationships.

Some frameworks require prior constitutional understanding before later frameworks become admissible.

Navigation shall therefore preserve dependency ordering.

---

## Observation 4

Every navigation decision shall preserve provenance.

A user shall always be capable of determining:

- where they entered;
- what framework they are studying;
- which constitutional authority governs that framework;
- why the framework appears in its current position.

---

## Observation 5

Navigation shall never imply constitutional equivalence.

Sequential proximity does not establish identical meaning.

Adjacency shall not be interpreted as constitutional identity.

---

## Observation 6

Navigation shall remain reconstructable.

Independent observers shall be capable of reproducing identical constitutional navigation using only admitted repository artifacts.

---

## Observation 7

Navigation shall preserve semantic boundaries.

Traversal between frameworks shall never alter the meaning of either framework.

---

## Observation 8

Morning Star functions as constitutional guidance.

Scientific authority remains entirely within the originating constitutional frameworks.

---

## Constitutional Finding

Constitutional navigation is itself a governed architectural system whose purpose is preserving admissible orientation while maintaining provenance, dependency ordering, semantic integrity, and framework independence.
