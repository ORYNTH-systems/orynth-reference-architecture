# Research Question

Execution ID:

MS-003

Status:

ACTIVE

Classification:

Constitutional Execution

---

## Primary Research Question

How shall Morning Star govern constitutional navigation into the ORYNTH ecosystem without replacing, collapsing, or reinterpreting the constitutional authority of the frameworks being entered?

---

## Secondary Questions

1. What constitutes an admissible entry point into the ORYNTH ecosystem?
2. How shall framework dependencies constrain navigation order?
3. How shall provenance remain visible throughout navigation?
4. How shall semantic boundaries be preserved during orientation?
5. How shall navigation state remain reconstructable?
6. How shall Morning Star distinguish orientation from constitutional authority?
7. What conditions render a navigation path constitutionally inadmissible?

---

## Governing Hypothesis

A constitutional navigation architecture can provide ordered entry into the ORYNTH ecosystem while preserving framework independence when navigation is governed by:

- explicit provenance;
- dependency-aware sequencing;
- semantic boundary preservation;
- constitutional admissibility;
- reconstructable navigation state.

---

## Null Condition

The navigation architecture fails constitutionally if it:

- obscures provenance;
- bypasses required dependencies;
- collapses framework identities;
- introduces unsupported equivalence;
- replaces originating constitutional authority;
- produces unreconstructable navigation states.

---

## Constitutional Constraint

Morning Star may govern the path of entry.

It may not govern the scientific truth, constitutional identity, or internal authority of the frameworks encountered through that path.
