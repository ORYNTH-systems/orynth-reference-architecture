## Verification Compared with Continuity

Verification confirms:

- evidence;
- criteria;
- integrity;
- admissibility.

Continuity preserves:

- identity;
- lineage;
- historical relationship;
- state connection.

Verification asks:

"Has integrity been demonstrated?"

Continuity asks:

"Is the current state still connected to its origin?"

---

## Stability Compared with Continuity

Stability preserves:

- coherent operation;
- reliable state conditions.

Continuity preserves:

- identity relationship;
- historical lineage.

A framework may remain stable without continuity if its origin cannot be reconstructed.

---

## Resilience Compared with Continuity

Resilience preserves integrity during disruption.

Continuity preserves identity across transitions.

Resilience answers:

"Can integrity survive stress?"

Continuity answers:

"Is this still the same framework?"

---

## Evolution Compared with Continuity

Evolution governs transformation.

Continuity determines whether transformation remains connected to originating identity.

Evolution without continuity may become replacement.

Continuity allows evolution without identity loss.

---

## Adaptation Compared with Continuity

Adaptation responds to conditions.

Continuity preserves the relationship between states before and after adaptation.

---

## Constitutional Boundary

MS-011 governs identity preservation across time.

It does not:

- prevent change;
- freeze frameworks;
- replace evolution;
- create authority.

---

## Final Comparative Finding

Constitutional Continuity is a distinct Morning Star capability governing preservation of identity relationships across all authorized states and transitions.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

A framework may change and remain continuous when its identity relationship remains reconstructable.
