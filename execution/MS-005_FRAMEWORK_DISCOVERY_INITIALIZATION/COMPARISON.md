## Identity Comparison

The reconstructed discovery architecture shall preserve the identical constitutional identity of the admitted architecture.

No framework shall gain, lose, inherit, or transfer constitutional authority through discovery.

---

## Structural Comparison

Equivalent discovery architectures shall preserve:

- identical discovery hierarchy;
- identical dependency graph;
- identical framework relationships;
- identical constitutional ordering;
- identical discovery pathways.

Structural deviation constitutes constitutional divergence.

---

## Semantic Comparison

Equivalent discovery shall preserve:

- terminology;
- constitutional meaning;
- provenance;
- framework attribution;
- semantic boundaries.

Discovery sequence shall not alter constitutional semantics.

---

## Behavioral Comparison

Equivalent discovery behavior shall:

- admit identical discovery paths;
- reject identical inadmissible discovery paths;
- preserve dependency constraints;
- produce identical reconstruction outcomes;
- preserve constitutional admissibility.

---

## Comparison Result

Constitutional equivalence exists only when every comparison criterion is satisfied.

Implementation differences are constitutionally admissible only when constitutional behavior remains invariant.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Morning Star Framework Discovery remains constitutionally equivalent only when identity, ordering, provenance, semantic integrity, dependency relationships, framework independence, reconstruction behavior, and constitutional authority remain unchanged.
