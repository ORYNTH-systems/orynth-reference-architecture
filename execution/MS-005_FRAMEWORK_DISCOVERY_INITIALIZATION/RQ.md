# Research Question

Execution ID:

MS-005

Status:

ACTIVE

Classification:

Constitutional Execution

---

## Primary Research Question

How shall Morning Star govern constitutional framework discovery within the ORYNTH ecosystem while preserving framework independence, provenance, semantic integrity, dependency ordering, and admissible entry into framework-specific study?

---

## Secondary Questions

1. What constitutes constitutional framework discovery?
2. How does discovery differ from orientation and navigation?
3. What conditions make a framework discoverable?
4. How shall dependency relationships constrain discovery order?
5. How shall provenance remain visible during discovery?
6. How shall discovery state remain reconstructable?
7. What conditions render framework discovery constitutionally inadmissible?

---

## Governing Hypothesis

A constitutionally governed discovery architecture can expose ORYNTH frameworks coherently when discovery preserves:

- constitutional identity;
- framework independence;
- dependency ordering;
- provenance;
- semantic boundaries;
- reconstructable discovery state.

---

## Null Condition

The discovery architecture fails constitutionally if it:

- exposes frameworks without required context;
- bypasses constitutional dependencies;
- obscures provenance;
- collapses framework identities;
- introduces unsupported semantic equivalence;
- modifies framework authority;
- produces unreconstructable discovery states.

---

## Constitutional Constraint

Morning Star may govern how frameworks become discoverable.

It shall not redefine, reinterpret, rank by scientific truth, replace, or supersede the constitutional authority, mathematics, evidence, governance, or evolution of any admitted ORYNTH framework.
