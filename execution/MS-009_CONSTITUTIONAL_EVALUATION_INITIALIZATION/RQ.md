# Research Question

Execution ID:

MS-009

Status:

ACTIVE

Classification:

Constitutional Execution

---

## Primary Research Question

How shall Morning Star govern constitutional evaluation within the ORYNTH ecosystem while assessing states, transitions, evidence, and outcomes without altering constitutional identity, framework independence, provenance, semantic integrity, dependency ordering, or reconstructability?

---

## Secondary Questions

1. What constitutes constitutional evaluation?
2. How does evaluation differ from application, comprehension, learning, discovery, and navigation?
3. What conditions make an evaluation admissible?
4. How shall evaluation preserve evidence provenance?
5. How shall evaluation distinguish assessment from authority?
6. How shall evaluation states remain reconstructable?
7. What conditions render constitutional evaluation inadmissible?

---

## Governing Hypothesis

A constitutionally governed evaluation architecture can assess states and outcomes when evaluation preserves:

- constitutional identity;
- framework independence;
- dependency ordering;
- provenance;
- semantic integrity;
- evidence integrity;
- reconstructable evaluation states.

---

## Null Condition

The evaluation architecture fails constitutionally if it:

- evaluates without sufficient evidence;
- removes provenance;
- confuses assessment with authority;
- collapses framework boundaries;
- bypasses dependencies;
- modifies constitutional meaning;
- produces unreconstructable evaluation states.

---

## Constitutional Constraint

Morning Star governs constitutional evaluation.

It may assess admissibility, integrity, and state conditions.

It shall not redefine, replace, supersede, or modify the constitutional authority, mathematics, evidence, governance, scientific conclusions, or evolution of any admitted ORYNTH framework.
