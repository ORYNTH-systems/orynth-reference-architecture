---

## Primary Research Question

Can Morning Star constitutionally establish an Entry Ontology that governs orientation into the ORYNTH ecosystem while preserving semantic identity, framework independence, constitutional boundaries, dependency integrity, provenance, and reconstructability?

---

## Secondary Questions

1. Can Entry Ontology remain distinct from scientific discovery?

2. Can orientation occur without modifying canonical meaning?

3. Are framework relationships explicitly defined without collapsing framework identity?

4. Can constitutional terminology be introduced without redefining existing ORYNTH concepts?

5. Can taxonomy, variables, and constitutional navigation remain reconstructable?

6. Does the Entry Ontology preserve dependency relationships among ORYNTH frameworks?

7. Does Morning Star function as constitutional initiation rather than scientific authority?

8. Can future ontology revisions occur through governed constitutional processes?

---

## Constitutional Hypothesis

The Entry Ontology is constitutionally admissible when it:

- introduces ORYNTH without redefining it;
- preserves framework independence;
- explicitly defines constitutional relationships;
- establishes governed terminology;
- establishes constitutional navigation;
- preserves provenance;
- remains historically reconstructable.

---

## Null Condition

The Entry Ontology shall not be constitutionally admitted if:

- framework identities become conflated;
- constitutional boundaries become ambiguous;
- Morning Star assumes scientific authority over existing frameworks;
- terminology silently redefines canonical meaning;
- dependency relationships become inconsistent;
- ontology cannot be reconstructed from preserved evidence.

---

## Evaluation Domain

This execution evaluates only the constitutional initialization of the Morning Star Entry Ontology.

It does not evaluate:

- empirical correctness;
- mathematical validity of ORYNTH frameworks;
- runtime implementation;
- institutional acceptance;
- future ecosystem expansion.

---

## Required Evidence

Evaluation shall rely upon:

- constitutional ontology artifacts;
- canonical Morning Star documents;
- governance records;
- framework relationship definitions;
- Git history;
- execution evidence;
- verification findings;
- reconstruction evidence.
