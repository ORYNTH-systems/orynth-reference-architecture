## Specification Purpose

This document defines the constitutional requirements for initializing the Morning Star Entry Ontology.

The specification governs how Morning Star introduces the ORYNTH ecosystem without absorbing, altering, or replacing the constitutional identity of the frameworks it presents.

---

## Constitutional Subject

Subject:

Morning Star Entry Ontology

Constitutional Role:

Governed orientation into the ORYNTH ecosystem

Primary Function:

Initiation into existing constitutional knowledge

---

## Required Ontology Components

The Entry Ontology shall define:

1. constitutional terminology;
2. foundational variables;
3. ecosystem taxonomy;
4. framework identities;
5. framework relationships;
6. dependency relationships;
7. constitutional navigation;
8. semantic boundaries;
9. provenance requirements;
10. reconstruction requirements.

---

## Required Framework Coverage

The Entry Ontology shall provide governed orientation into:

- Unified Agency Architecture;
- Integrity Preservation Theory;
- Adaptive Orchestration Management System;
- Execution Integrity Protocol;
- Constraint-Integrated Glyphic Control System;
- Planetary Psychology Atlas;
- Constitutional Game Runtime;
- Uprising Narrative Substrate.

Additional frameworks may be admitted through governed revision or amendment.

---

## Framework Independence Requirements

Each framework shall retain:

- independent constitutional identity;
- independent scientific scope;
- independent variables;
- independent claims;
- independent evidence requirements;
- independent governance where applicable.

Morning Star shall not silently merge framework identities.

---

## Navigation Requirements

The Entry Ontology shall allow an initiate to determine:

- where they are entering the ecosystem;
- what each framework governs;
- how frameworks relate;
- which dependencies exist;
- which concepts are shared;
- which concepts remain framework-specific;
- where canonical authority resides;
- what sequence of study is constitutionally admissible.

---

## Boundary Requirements

Morning Star shall remain distinct from:

- foundational scientific frameworks;
- applied systems;
- execution runtimes;
- narrative substrates;
- institutional governance;
- publication repositories.

Orientation shall not be represented as scientific authority.

---

## Terminology Requirements

Terminology shall:

- preserve existing canonical meanings;
- identify framework-specific meanings;
- distinguish shared terms from local terms;
- preserve ambiguity where unresolved;
- prohibit silent semantic substitution;
- preserve provenance for later revisions.

---

## Relationship Requirements

Each framework relationship shall identify:

- source framework;
- target framework;
- relationship type;
- dependency direction;
- constitutional significance;
- semantic limitations;
- provenance.

Relationship definitions shall not imply equivalence unless equivalence is explicitly established.

---

## Dependency Requirements

Dependencies shall be:

- explicit;
- directional where applicable;
- reconstructable;
- distinguishable from influence;
- distinguishable from thematic similarity;
- distinguishable from constitutional authority.

---

## Provenance Requirements

Every admitted ontology element shall remain traceable to:

- a canonical artifact;
- an approved governance artifact;
- an admitted execution record;
- or an explicitly marked provisional source.

Unsupported ontology claims shall not be admitted as canonical.

---

## Reconstruction Requirements

The Entry Ontology shall be reconstructable from:

- repository artifacts;
- Git history;
- version tags;
- execution evidence;
- relationship records;
- terminology records;
- governance records.

---

## Placeholder Requirements

No admitted ontology artifact may contain unresolved structural placeholders including:

- TODO
- TBD
- FIXME
- INSERT HERE
- PLACEHOLDER

Required sections shall not remain empty.

---

## Verification Requirements

Verification shall evaluate:

- ontology completeness;
- navigation completeness;
- framework independence;
- boundary preservation;
- dependency preservation;
- provenance;
- reconstructability;
- placeholder absence.

Each condition shall receive:

- PASS
- FAIL
- INDETERMINATE
- NOT APPLICABLE

---

## Admission Rule

The Entry Ontology shall be classified as ENTRY ONTOLOGY INITIALIZED only when all identity-bearing, boundary-bearing, dependency-bearing, and reconstruction-bearing requirements pass.

---

## Failure Conditions

Initialization shall fail when:

- required ontology components are absent;
- framework identities are conflated;
- Morning Star assumes scientific authority;
- relationships are materially ambiguous;
- dependencies cannot be reconstructed;
- provenance is insufficient;
- unresolved structural placeholders remain.

---

## Indeterminate Conditions

The result shall be INDETERMINATE when:

- source authority cannot be established;
- framework relationships conflict;
- evidence is incomplete;
- repository history is unavailable;
- semantic identity cannot be verified.

---

## Required Final Determination

The execution shall produce exactly one final determination:

- ENTRY ONTOLOGY INITIALIZED
- PROVISIONALLY INITIALIZED
- INITIALIZATION INCOMPLETE
- INDETERMINATE
- RECONSTRUCTION REQUIRED
