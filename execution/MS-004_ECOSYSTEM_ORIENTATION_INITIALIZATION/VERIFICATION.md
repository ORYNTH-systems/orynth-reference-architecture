## Purpose

This document defines the constitutional verification procedure for the Morning Star Ecosystem Orientation Architecture.

Verification confirms that ecosystem orientation preserves constitutional identity while establishing coherent understanding of the ORYNTH ecosystem.

---

## Verification Objectives

The verification process shall confirm that:

- ecosystem orientation is deterministic;
- constitutional identity is preserved;
- framework independence is preserved;
- dependency ordering is maintained;
- provenance remains intact;
- semantic boundaries remain unchanged;
- reconstruction produces constitutionally equivalent orientation.

---

## Verification Criteria

Execution shall PASS only when all of the following conditions are satisfied:

- orientation introduces every framework without modifying its authority;
- dependency ordering remains constitutionally admissible;
- provenance remains explicit and reconstructable;
- semantic integrity is preserved;
- orientation remains deterministic;
- constitutional invariants remain satisfied.

---

## Verification Procedure

1. Verify the Constitutional Model.
2. Verify governance artifacts.
3. Verify ontology artifacts.
4. Verify navigation architecture.
5. Verify ecosystem orientation architecture.
6. Verify dependency ordering.
7. Verify provenance preservation.
8. Verify semantic boundary preservation.

---

## Failure Conditions

Verification shall FAIL if:

- framework authority is modified;
- dependency ordering is violated;
- provenance becomes ambiguous;
- semantic drift is introduced;
- orientation cannot be reconstructed;
- constitutional identity changes.

## Expected Result

Successful verification demonstrates that Morning Star provides constitutionally admissible ecosystem orientation while preserving complete framework independence and constitutional integrity.
