## Evidence Objective

This execution establishes the constitutional evidence required to demonstrate that verification within the Morning Star architecture remains complete, traceable, evidence-based, provenance-preserving, and constitutionally governed.

---

## Evidence Category 1 — Verification Provenance

Every constitutional verification event shall preserve:

- verification target;
- originating framework;
- originating constitutional authority;
- evidence sources;
- verification criteria;
- execution history;
- verification result.

---

## Evidence Category 2 — Evidence Chain Integrity

Evidence shall demonstrate:

- source attribution;
- evidence availability;
- evidence completeness;
- evidence continuity;
- evidence reconstruction capability.

Verification shall not exceed the evidence chain available.

---

## Evidence Category 3 — Verification Criteria Integrity

Evidence shall demonstrate that verification criteria remain:

- explicit;
- traceable;
- consistently applied;
- constitutionally bounded.

---

## Evidence Category 4 — Execution Continuity

Evidence shall demonstrate continuity across:

- constitutional initialization;
- ontology establishment;
- navigation;
- orientation;
- discovery;
- learning;
- comprehension;
- application;
- evaluation;
- verification.

---

## Evidence Category 5 — Reconstruction Capability

Evidence shall demonstrate that equivalent constitutional inputs reproduce:

- identical verification state;
- identical evidence relationships;
- identical verification criteria;
- identical admissibility result.

---

## Evidence Category 6 — Authority Preservation

Evidence shall demonstrate that verification never modifies:

- framework authority;
- mathematical models;
- governance;
- evidence;
- scientific conclusions;
- constitutional evolution.

---

## Constitutional Evidence Requirement

A verification state is constitutionally admissible only when confirmation remains attributable, evidence-supported, context-preserving, semantically intact, dependency-valid, and independently reconstructable.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Verification confirms constitutional integrity.

Verification does not create constitutional authority.
