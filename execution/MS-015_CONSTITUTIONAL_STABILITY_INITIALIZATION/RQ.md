---

## Primary Research Question

How shall Morning Star govern constitutional stability within the ORYNTH ecosystem while maintaining coherent operation across adaptation, evolution, resilience, and future transitions without reducing flexibility or losing framework identity?

---

## Secondary Questions

1. What constitutes constitutional stability?
2. How does stability differ from resilience, continuity, and adaptation?
3. What conditions indicate stable operation?
4. How shall stability preserve dynamic capability?
5. How shall unstable states be distinguished from evolutionary transitions?
6. How shall stabilization remain reconstructable?
7. What conditions indicate constitutional instability?

---

## Governing Hypothesis

A constitutionally governed stability architecture can preserve coherent operation when stability maintains:

- identity invariants;
- continuity;
- provenance;
- semantic integrity;
- operational coherence;
- reconstruction capability.

---

## Null Condition

The stability architecture fails constitutionally if it:

- confuses stability with immobility;
- prevents authorized evolution;
- stabilizes identity loss;
- removes provenance;
- creates unreconstructable states.

---

## Constitutional Constraint

Morning Star governs constitutional stability.

It may preserve coherent operation.

It shall not independently prevent evolution, authorize modification, or redefine originating framework authority.
