## Primary Research Question

How shall Morning Star govern constitutional learning within the ORYNTH ecosystem while preserving constitutional identity, framework independence, provenance, semantic integrity, dependency ordering, and admissible knowledge acquisition?

---

## Secondary Questions

1. What constitutes constitutional learning?
2. How does learning differ from discovery, orientation, and navigation?
3. What conditions make constitutional learning admissible?
4. How shall dependency relationships constrain learning order?
5. How shall provenance remain visible throughout learning?
6. How shall constitutional learning remain reconstructable?
7. What conditions render constitutional learning constitutionally inadmissible?

---

## Governing Hypothesis

A constitutionally governed learning architecture can establish coherent constitutional understanding when learning preserves:

- constitutional identity;
- framework independence;
- dependency ordering;
- provenance;
- semantic integrity;
- reconstructable learning state.

---

## Null Condition

The learning architecture fails constitutionally if it:

- bypasses constitutional prerequisites;
- obscures provenance;
- collapses framework identities;
- introduces unsupported semantic equivalence;
- modifies framework authority;
- produces unreconstructable learning states.

---

## Constitutional Constraint

Morning Star governs constitutional learning.

It shall not redefine, reinterpret, replace, supersede, or modify the constitutional authority, mathematics, evidence, governance, scientific conclusions, or constitutional evolution of any admitted ORYNTH framework.
