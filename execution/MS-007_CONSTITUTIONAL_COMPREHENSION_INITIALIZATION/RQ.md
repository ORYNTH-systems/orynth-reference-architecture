---

## Primary Research Question

How shall Morning Star govern constitutional comprehension within the ORYNTH ecosystem while allowing acquired knowledge to become integrated understanding without altering framework identity, authority, provenance, semantic integrity, dependency ordering, or reconstructability?

---

## Secondary Questions

1. What constitutes constitutional comprehension?
2. How does comprehension differ from learning, discovery, orientation, and navigation?
3. How shall learned concepts be integrated without semantic collapse?
4. How shall relationships between concepts be formed while preserving provenance?
5. How shall comprehension respect framework-specific boundaries?
6. How shall comprehension states remain reconstructable?
7. What conditions render constitutional comprehension inadmissible?

---

## Governing Hypothesis

A constitutionally governed comprehension architecture can transform acquired constitutional knowledge into coherent understanding when comprehension preserves:

- constitutional identity;
- framework independence;
- dependency ordering;
- provenance;
- semantic integrity;
- reconstructable comprehension states.

---

## Null Condition

The comprehension architecture fails constitutionally if it:

- merges independent framework identities;
- removes provenance;
- creates unsupported relationships;
- bypasses dependencies;
- reinterprets canonical meaning;
- modifies framework authority;
- produces unreconstructable comprehension states.

---

## Constitutional Constraint

Morning Star governs constitutional comprehension.

It may organize relationships between learned concepts.

It shall not redefine the meaning, authority, mathematics, evidence, governance, or evolution of any admitted ORYNTH framework.
