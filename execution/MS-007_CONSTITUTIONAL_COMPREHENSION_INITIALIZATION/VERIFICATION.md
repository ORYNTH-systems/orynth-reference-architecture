## Verification Objective

Verify that the Morning Star Constitutional Comprehension Architecture governs the integration of learned constitutional knowledge while preserving identity, provenance, semantic integrity, dependency ordering, framework independence, and reconstructability.

---

## Verification Condition 1 — Learning State Validity

PASS when:

- all integrated knowledge originates from admitted learning states;
- learning provenance is preserved;
- prerequisite learning states are verified;
- no unadmitted knowledge enters comprehension.

FAIL when comprehension contains unsupported or unverified knowledge states.

---

## Verification Condition 2 — Relationship Integrity

PASS when:

- all concept relationships are explicitly attributable;
- relationship formation preserves framework boundaries;
- contextual qualifiers remain intact;
- relationships do not imply unsupported equivalence.

FAIL when relationships collapse independent constitutional meanings.

---

## Verification Condition 3 — Provenance Preservation

PASS when every comprehension relationship preserves:

- originating framework;
- originating authority;
- source artifact;
- learning history;
- dependency chain.

FAIL when provenance becomes incomplete, ambiguous, or unreconstructable.

---

## Verification Condition 4 — Semantic Integrity

PASS when comprehension preserves:

- canonical terminology;
- canonical definitions;
- framework-specific meaning;
- constitutional distinctions.

FAIL when comprehension introduces:

- semantic collapse;
- unauthorized reinterpretation;
- terminology substitution;
- unsupported equivalence.

---

## Verification Condition 5 — Framework Independence

PASS when comprehension increases understanding without changing:

- framework authority;
- framework identity;
- framework scope;
- framework governance.

FAIL when comprehension creates authority transfer or framework merging.

---

## Verification Condition 6 — Deterministic Comprehension

PASS when equivalent constitutional inputs produce:

- identical comprehension state;
- identical concept relationships;
- identical provenance;
- identical admissibility outcome.

FAIL when equivalent inputs produce materially different constitutional states.

---

## Verification Condition 7 — Reconstruction Completeness

PASS when an independent observer can reproduce the comprehension state using:

- admitted learning states;
- provenance records;
- dependency records;
- constitutional artifacts;
- governing comprehension rules.

FAIL when hidden assumptions or unavailable information are required.
