## Continuity Compared with Evolution

Continuity preserves:

- identity;
- lineage;
- historical relationship.

Evolution governs:

- transformation;
- expansion;
- structured change.

Continuity determines whether evolution remains connected to origin.

---

## Adaptation Compared with Evolution

Adaptation responds to conditions.

Evolution changes structure or capability while preserving identity.

An adaptive response may occur without evolution.

---

## Resilience Compared with Evolution

Resilience preserves integrity during stress.

Evolution transforms the framework while maintaining constitutional relationship.

---

## Stability Compared with Evolution

Stability maintains coherent operation.

Evolution introduces governed transformation.

---

## Modification Compared with Evolution

Modification changes an artifact.

Evolution requires classification of whether identity remains preserved.

---

## Replacement Compared with Evolution

Replacement creates a new identity relationship.

Evolution preserves identity through transformation.

---

## Constitutional Boundary

MS-012 governs transformation classification.

It does not:

- create authority;
- redefine frameworks;
- replace governance;
- alter evidence;
- modify constitutional ownership.

---

## Final Comparative Finding

Constitutional Evolution is the capability that determines whether transformation remains connected to originating identity.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Change becomes evolution only when identity continuity remains reconstructable.
