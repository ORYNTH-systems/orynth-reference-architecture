# Research Question

Execution ID:

MS-012

Status:

ACTIVE

Classification:

Constitutional Execution

---

## Primary Research Question

How shall Morning Star govern constitutional evolution within the ORYNTH ecosystem while enabling authorized transformation, extension, and development without losing framework identity, continuity, provenance, semantic integrity, invariant relationships, or reconstructability?

---

## Secondary Questions

1. What constitutes constitutional evolution?
2. How does evolution differ from continuity, revision, replacement, and modification?
3. What properties must remain invariant for a framework to remain itself?
4. How shall authorized changes preserve historical lineage?
5. How shall evolution maintain provenance across transitions?
6. How shall evolutionary states remain reconstructable?
7. What conditions classify a change as continuation versus emergence of a separate framework?

---

## Governing Hypothesis

A constitutionally governed evolution architecture can enable structured transformation when evolution preserves:

- identity invariants;
- historical lineage;
- provenance chains;
- semantic boundaries;
- dependency relationships;
- reconstruction capability.

---

## Null Condition

The evolution architecture fails constitutionally if it:

- permits change without lineage;
- modifies identity without classification;
- removes provenance;
- collapses separate frameworks;
- confuses evolution with replacement;
- produces unreconstructable transitions.

---

## Constitutional Constraint

Morning Star governs constitutional evolution.

It may define how change is classified, preserved, and reconstructed.

It shall not independently authorize changes to originating frameworks, scientific conclusions, mathematical models, governance authority, or constitutional ownership.
