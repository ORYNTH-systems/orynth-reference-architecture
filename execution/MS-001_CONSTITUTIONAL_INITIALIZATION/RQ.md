---

## Primary Research Question

Can Morning Star be constitutionally initialized as the governed entry architecture of the ORYNTH ecosystem while preserving semantic identity, framework differentiation, dependency integrity, provenance, uncertainty, reconstruction capability, authority boundaries, and stewardship continuity?

---

## Secondary Questions

1. Does the Canonical Model define a complete constitutional object architecture?

2. Does the governance layer preserve constitutional continuity without redefining canonical meaning?

3. Are authority, stewardship, revision, amendment, versioning, and publication responsibilities explicitly separated?

4. Can the constitutional baseline be reconstructed from repository evidence?

5. Does the initialization preserve historical continuity across commits, tags, governance records, and execution artifacts?

6. Are semantic admissibility and execution authority constitutionally separated?

7. Are future revisions constrained by explicit governance procedures?

8. Does the repository contain sufficient evidence to verify constitutional initialization?

---

## Constitutional Hypothesis

Morning Star may be considered constitutionally initialized when:

- the Canonical Model is structurally complete;
- constitutional identity is explicitly declared;
- governance responsibilities are bounded;
- revision and amendment procedures are established;
- authority and stewardship remain distinct;
- version and publication history are preserved;
- initialization evidence is reconstructable;
- no unresolved structural placeholder remains in the admitted baseline.

---

## Null Condition

Morning Star shall not be considered constitutionally initialized if any of the following conditions remain true:

- canonical identity is undefined;
- constitutional boundaries are materially incomplete;
- governance authority is ambiguous;
- stewardship continuity is absent;
- revision pathways are undefined;
- historical evidence cannot be reconstructed;
- publication status is presented as authority without constitutional admission;
- semantic admissibility is conflated with execution authority.

---

## Evaluation Domain

This execution evaluates constitutional initialization only.

It does not evaluate:

- empirical validity of all ORYNTH frameworks;
- implementation correctness of derived systems;
- runtime execution eligibility;
- institutional adoption;
- public acceptance;
- universal semantic agreement.
