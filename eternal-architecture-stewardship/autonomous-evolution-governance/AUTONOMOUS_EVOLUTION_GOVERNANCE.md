# Autonomous Evolution Governance

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework used to govern autonomous evolutionary processes across the ORYNTH architecture.

---

## Governance Principle

Autonomous evolution governance enables continuous architectural advancement through controlled evaluation, authorized transition, validation, and preservation of evolutionary lineage.

Autonomous evolution operates within defined governance boundaries rather than independent uncontrolled change.

---

## Governance Domains

Includes:

- evolutionary authority;
- transition evaluation;
- autonomous decision support;
- constraint preservation;
- governance validation;
- adaptation management;
- historical continuity.

---

## Governance Lifecycle


IDENTIFY EVOLUTION CONDITION

↓

ASSESS ARCHITECTURAL IMPACT

↓

EVALUATE GOVERNANCE REQUIREMENTS

↓

DEFINE AUTHORIZED TRANSITION

↓

APPLY CONTROLLED EVOLUTION

↓

VALIDATE RESULTING STATE

↓

PRESERVE EVOLUTION RECORD

↓

ENABLE FUTURE GOVERNANCE CYCLES


---

## Principle

Autonomous evolution governance ensures ORYNTH can advance continuously while preserving accountability, architectural identity, and controlled transformation.
