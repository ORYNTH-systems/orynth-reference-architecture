# Institutional Governance Model

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the institutional governance model required to maintain the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture as a long-term reference system.

---

## Governance Principle

Institutional governance provides the structure through which architectural decisions, preservation responsibilities, and future stewardship activities remain accountable.

Governance maintains continuity between the originating architecture and future custodians.

---

## Governance Domains

Includes:

- decision authority;
- architectural oversight;
- revision governance;
- certification responsibility;
- stakeholder coordination;
- public reference management.

---

## Governance Lifecycle


ARCHITECTURE REFERENCE STATE

↓

GOVERNANCE BODY ESTABLISHED

↓

RESPONSIBILITIES ASSIGNED

↓

DECISIONS GOVERNED

↓

CHANGES REVIEWED

↓

CONTINUITY PRESERVED


---

## Governance Requirements

Institutional governance must preserve:

- architectural identity;
- constitutional principles;
- authority boundaries;
- decision traceability;
- historical continuity.

---

## Principle

The Institutional Governance Model ensures the architecture remains accountable beyond its initial creation.
