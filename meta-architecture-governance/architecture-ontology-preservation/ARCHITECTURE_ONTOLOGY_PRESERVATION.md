# Architecture Ontology Preservation

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the ontology preservation framework used to maintain the identity, meaning, structure, and relationships of ORYNTH architecture components across continuous evolution.

---

## Ontology Preservation Principle

Architecture ontology preservation ensures that ORYNTH entities, concepts, relationships, boundaries, and definitions remain coherent as the architecture expands.

Evolution may transform implementation and expression while preserving foundational meaning.

---

## Ontology Domains

Includes:

- architectural entities;
- conceptual definitions;
- system relationships;
- dependency structures;
- governance concepts;
- capability classifications;
- lifecycle states;
- reference boundaries.

---

## Ontology Preservation Lifecycle


IDENTIFY ARCHITECTURAL ENTITY

↓

↓

MAP RELATIONSHIPS AND DEPENDENCIES

↓

VALIDATE ONTOLOGICAL CONSISTENCY

↓

PRESERVE REFERENCE IDENTITY

↓

TRACK EVOLUTIONARY CHANGES

↓

MAINTAIN CONTINUITY


---

## Principle

Architecture ontology preservation ensures ORYNTH evolves without semantic fragmentation, identity loss, or uncontrolled reinterpretation.
