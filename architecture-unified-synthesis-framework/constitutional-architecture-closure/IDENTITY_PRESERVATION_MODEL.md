# Identity Preservation Model

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define how ORYNTH preserves identity through architectural change.

---

## Identity Principle

Identity is preserved when transformation modifies implementation while maintaining constitutional relationships.

---

## Identity Preservation Conditions

A change preserves identity when it maintains:

- original purpose;
- constitutional constraints;
- dependency relationships;
- semantic boundaries;
- governance authority;
- historical continuity.

---

## Identity Evaluation Model


PROPOSED CHANGE

↓

IDENTITY IMPACT ANALYSIS

↓

INVARIANT CHECK

↓

GOVERNANCE REVIEW

↓

TRANSFORMATION APPROVAL

↓

UPDATED ARCHITECTURE STATE


---

## Principle

The Identity Preservation Model enables ORYNTH to evolve while remaining the same architecture through governed continuity.
