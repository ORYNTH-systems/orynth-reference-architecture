# Adaptive Evolution Governance

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the governance framework used to manage ORYNTH architectural evolution, adaptation decisions, and controlled transformation pathways.

---

## Evolution Governance Principle

Adaptive evolution governance enables architecture to change while preserving identity, continuity, traceability, and alignment with foundational constraints.

Evolution requires direction, validation, and historical preservation.

---

## Governance Domains

Includes:

- evolution assessment;
- change classification;
- architectural continuity;
- transition management;
- validation processes;
- governance constraints;
- adaptation pathways;
- historical preservation.

---

## Evolution Governance Lifecycle


IDENTIFY EVOLUTION CONDITION

↓

ASSESS ARCHITECTURAL IMPACT

↓

CLASSIFY CHANGE TYPE

↓

EVALUATE GOVERNANCE REQUIREMENTS

↓

APPROVE EVOLUTION PATHWAY

↓

IMPLEMENT CONTROLLED TRANSITION

↓

VALIDATE RESULTING STATE

↓

PRESERVE EVOLUTION HISTORY

## Principle

Adaptive evolution governance ensures ORYNTH can transform through intentional, traceable, and validated architectural progression.
