# Discovery State Modeling

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework for representing discovery states within ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Discovery Principle

Discovery begins where existing knowledge reaches its current boundary.

A discovery state represents the transition between:

- known information;
- unexplored possibility;
- emerging understanding;
- validated discovery.

---

## Discovery Domains

Includes:

- architectural relationships;
- emergent capabilities;
- unexplored patterns;
- unresolved questions;
- future knowledge states.

---

## Discovery Lifecycle

UNKNOWN CONDITION IDENTIFIED

↓

OBSERVATION COLLECTED

↓

PATTERN OR POSSIBILITY FORMULATED

↓

DISCOVERY EVALUATED

↓

DISCOVERY VALIDATED

↓

KNOWLEDGE STATE EXPANDED

---

## Discovery Requirements

Discovery states must preserve:

- originating observation;
- exploration context;
- evidence relationship;
- uncertainty conditions;
- validation status;
- transition history.

---

## Principle

Discovery State Modeling ensures unknown conditions can be explored without being confused with established knowledge.
