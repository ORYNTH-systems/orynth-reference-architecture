# Framework Relationships

Status: ACTIVE

Version: 0.1.0

Classification: Constitutional Ontology

---

## Purpose

This document defines the constitutional relationships among the primary ORYNTH frameworks.

Relationships describe organization, interaction, and constitutional dependency.

Relationships do not imply scientific equivalence.

---

## Relationship Types

The Entry Ontology recognizes the following constitutional relationship classes:

- Orientation
- Foundation
- Dependency
- Application
- Extension
- Integration
- Narrative
- Governance

---

## Relationship Principles

Every constitutional relationship shall preserve:

- framework independence;
- semantic boundaries;
- provenance;
- reconstructability;
- governance compatibility.

Relationships shall remain directional where direction exists.

Undirected relationships shall be explicitly declared.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Framework relationships organize the ORYNTH ecosystem.

They shall never be interpreted as transferring constitutional authority from one framework to another.
