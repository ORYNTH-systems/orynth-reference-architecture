# Semantic Boundaries

Status: ACTIVE

Version: 0.1.0

Classification: Constitutional Ontology

---

## Purpose

This document defines the semantic boundaries governing the Morning Star Entry Ontology.

Semantic boundaries preserve constitutional identity across the ORYNTH ecosystem.

They prevent unauthorized transfer, substitution, or absorption of meaning between independent frameworks.

---

## Boundary Principles

Semantic boundaries shall preserve:

- constitutional identity;
- framework independence;
- terminology integrity;
- provenance;
- reconstructability;
- governance compatibility.

---

## Boundary Rules

Morning Star shall:

- introduce constitutional concepts;
- identify originating frameworks;
- preserve original terminology;
- distinguish shared concepts from framework-specific concepts;
- preserve uncertainty where constitutional meaning remains unresolved.

Morning Star shall not:

- redefine scientific terminology;
- absorb framework identities;
- create implicit equivalence between frameworks;
- replace canonical documentation.

---

## Framework Boundaries

Each constitutional framework maintains authority over:

- its terminology;
- variables;
- mathematical models;
- evidence;
- admissibility rules;
- constitutional scope.

Morning Star introduces these authorities without replacing them.

---

## Shared Concepts

A concept may appear in multiple frameworks.

Shared usage shall not imply identical meaning.

Each occurrence shall preserve provenance and framework attribution.

---

## Semantic Drift

Semantic drift occurs when terminology gradually changes meaning without constitutional authorization.

Semantic drift shall be prevented through:

- provenance preservation;
- governance review;
- constitutional revision procedures;
- amendment procedures.

---

## Boundary Violations

Examples of semantic boundary violations include:

- redefining framework terminology;
- merging independent framework identities;
- transferring constitutional authority without amendment;
- introducing unsupported equivalence;
- removing provenance.

Such changes require constitutional amendment before admission.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Semantic boundaries preserve the independent constitutional identity of every ORYNTH framework.

Morning Star shall remain an orientation architecture and shall never become the constitutional authority for the scientific frameworks it introduces.
