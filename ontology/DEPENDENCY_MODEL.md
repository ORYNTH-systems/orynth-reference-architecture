# Dependency Model

Status: ACTIVE

Version: 0.1.0

Classification: Constitutional Ontology

---

## Purpose

This document defines the constitutional dependency model used by the Morning Star Entry Ontology.

Dependencies describe constitutional prerequisites for orientation.

Dependencies do not establish scientific superiority or constitutional authority.

---

## Dependency Principles

Every dependency shall preserve:

- framework independence;
- semantic boundaries;
- provenance;
- reconstructability;
- governance compatibility.

Dependencies identify constitutional ordering.

They do not redefine framework identity.

---

## Dependency Classes

### Orientation Dependency

Definition:

Required for constitutional understanding of ecosystem organization.

Example:

Morning Star → ORYNTH

---

### Foundational Dependency

Definition:

A framework depends upon foundational constitutional concepts established elsewhere.

Example:

AOMS → IPT

---

### Application Dependency

Definition:

An applied system depends upon one or more foundational frameworks.

Examples:

Planetary Psychology Atlas

Constitutional Game Runtime

↓

Foundational Science

---

### Narrative Dependency

Definition:

Narrative substrates depend upon constitutional concepts for coherent interpretation.

Narrative does not become constitutional authority.

Example:

Uprising

↓

Foundational Science

## Dependency Rules

Dependencies shall:

- remain explicit;
- preserve direction;
- preserve provenance;
- remain reconstructable;
- avoid circular constitutional authority.

Dependencies may be revised only through constitutional governance.

---

## Dependency Constraints

A dependency shall never:

- merge framework identities;
- replace constitutional authority;
- imply scientific equivalence;
- silently redefine terminology;
- invalidate preserved provenance.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

The constitutional dependency model governs ecosystem ordering only.

Dependency relationships shall never be interpreted as transferring constitutional identity or authority between ORYNTH frameworks.
