# Terminology

Status: ACTIVE

Version: 0.1.0

Classification: Constitutional Ontology

---

## Purpose

This document establishes the constitutional terminology used by the Morning Star Entry Ontology.

Terminology governs orientation into ORYNTH.

It does not redefine terminology governed by individual constitutional frameworks.

---

## Entry

Definition:

The constitutionally governed point at which an initiate begins orientation into the ORYNTH ecosystem.

---

## Initiate

Definition:

An individual entering the ORYNTH ecosystem for the purpose of understanding its constitutional organization, relationships, terminology, and frameworks.

The term carries no implication regarding expertise, authority, or completion.

---

## Entry Ontology

Definition:

The governed semantic architecture that introduces an initiate to the constitutional structure of ORYNTH while preserving the independence of every framework.

---

## Orientation

Definition:

The constitutional process of introducing ecosystem organization without altering the scientific authority of the systems being introduced.

---

## Constitutional Framework

Definition:

An independently governed body of constitutional knowledge possessing its own scope, terminology, variables, evidence, and admissibility requirements.

Examples include:

- Unified Agency Architecture
- Integrity Preservation Theory
- Adaptive Orchestration Management System
- Execution Integrity Protocol
- Constraint-Integrated Glyphic Control System
- Planetary Psychology Atlas

---

## Framework Relationship

Definition:

A constitutionally defined connection between two frameworks.

Relationships describe organization.

Relationships do not imply equivalence.

---

## Dependency

Definition:

A constitutional relationship in which understanding one framework requires or benefits from understanding another.

Dependencies are directional unless explicitly declared otherwise.

---

## Constitutional Navigation

Definition:

The governed process by which an initiate moves through ORYNTH while preserving constitutional ordering and framework independence.

---

## Semantic Boundary

Definition:

The constitutional limit beyond which one framework may not silently redefine the concepts of another framework.

---

## Provenance

Definition:

The preserved constitutional origin of every admitted concept, definition, relationship, and dependency.

Every ontology element shall remain attributable to its originating framework.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Terminology introduced by Morning Star shall preserve semantic continuity across the ORYNTH ecosystem without replacing the constitutional authority of the originating frameworks.
