## Purpose

This document defines the constitutional taxonomy of the ORYNTH ecosystem as presented through the Morning Star Entry Ontology.

The taxonomy governs organization.

It does not redefine the constitutional authority of the frameworks it classifies.

## Taxonomic Relationships

The taxonomy defines organizational placement.

It does not define scientific superiority.

It does not define chronological creation.

It does not define implementation priority.

---

## Taxonomic Admission

New constitutional frameworks may be admitted only through governed constitutional revision or amendment.

Admission requires preservation of:

- constitutional identity;
- semantic boundaries;
- provenance;
- reconstructability.

---

## Taxonomic Invariants

The taxonomy shall preserve:

- independent framework identities;
- constitutional boundaries;
- governance compatibility;
- reconstructable organization;
- semantic continuity.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

The ORYNTH taxonomy is an organizational model.

Taxonomic placement shall never be interpreted as scientific authority over the frameworks being organized.
