# Recursive Governance Learning

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework used to enable recursive learning from ORYNTH governance processes, architectural decisions, and evolutionary outcomes.

---

## Recursive Learning Principle

Recursive governance learning enables governance systems to evaluate previous states, incorporate validated knowledge, and refine future governance processes while preserving architectural identity and constraints.

Learning occurs through structured feedback.

---

## Learning Domains

Includes:

- governance decision analysis;
- historical state comparison;
- feedback integration;
- transition evaluation;
- constraint refinement;
- process improvement;
- knowledge preservation;
- future governance readiness.

## Learning Constraints

Recursive governance learning preserves:

- decision provenance;
- architecture identity;
- semantic boundaries;
- uncertainty representation;
- validation evidence;
- governance continuity.

---

## Principle

Recursive Governance Learning enables ORYNTH to improve governance capability through structured feedback while maintaining integrity, traceability, and architectural coherence.
