# Autonomous Capability Coordination

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework for coordinating autonomous capabilities within ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Coordination Principle

Autonomous capability coordination determines how available capabilities are aligned with:

- agency objectives;
- environmental conditions;
- governance constraints;
- execution requirements;
- expected outcomes.

Capabilities are coordinated through eligibility and context, not simple availability.

---

## Coordination Domains

Includes:

- capability selection;
- capability sequencing;
- resource allocation;
- dependency management;
- execution coordination;
- outcome monitoring.

---

AGENCY STATE IDENTIFIED

↓

↓

EXECUTION PATHWAY GENERATED

↓

GOVERNED COORDINATION OCCURS

↓

OUTCOME STATE UPDATED


---

## Coordination Requirements

Capability coordination must preserve:

- capability identity;
- execution conditions;
- dependencies;
- governance requirements;
- environmental context;
- outcome relationships.

---

## Principle

Autonomous Capability Coordination ensures agency operates through structured capability relationships rather than uncontrolled execution.
