## 1. Specification Purpose

This document specifies the constitutional mechanisms required for Morning Star to govern semantic admissibility throughout the ORYNTH ecosystem.

Morning Star shall operate as the ORYNTH Constitutional Entry Architecture.

It shall govern the transition from first contact with ORYNTH through increasingly responsible forms of participation while preserving:

- canonical meaning;
- framework identity;
- constitutional dependency order;
- source traceability;
- explicit uncertainty;
- admissible interpretive diversity;
- ecosystem continuity.

Morning Star shall not replace the constitutional authority of the frameworks it interprets.

---

## 2. Constitutional Governance Domain

Morning Star governs semantic admissibility.

Semantic admissibility is the condition in which an interpretation:

1. preserves the identity of its constitutional subject;
2. remains traceable to authoritative source artifacts;
3. respects applicable dependency relationships;
4. distinguishes established meaning from inference and uncertainty;
5. does not introduce unauthorized constitutional claims;
6. remains sufficiently coherent for the observer's intended participation role.

Semantic admissibility does not require identical wording, representation, learning path, or personal interpretation.

It requires constitutionally equivalent preservation of governed meaning.

## 4. Participation Roles

Morning Star shall distinguish the following participation roles.

### R0 — Observer

A person or system encountering ORYNTH without established constitutional competence.

### R1 — Oriented Observer

An observer who can identify ORYNTH's principal architectural domains and distinguish them at a foundational level.

### R2 — Interpreter

A participant capable of reconstructing canonical meanings and framework relationships from authoritative sources.

### R3 — Practitioner

A participant capable of applying bounded ORYNTH mechanisms without materially altering their constitutional identity.

### R4 — Implementer

A participant capable of constructing derived systems while preserving constitutional dependencies, provenance, and scope.

### R5 — Contributor

A participant capable of proposing new interpretations, extensions, artifacts, or research findings with explicit constitutional status.

### R6 — Representative

A participant authorized to communicate or formally represent specified ORYNTH subjects within bounded authority.

### R7 — Constitutional Steward

A participant entrusted with governed interpretation, continuity, revision review, succession, or constitutional preservation.

Roles shall not be treated as measures of personal worth or intelligence.

Role admissibility shall be determined by demonstrated constitutional competence for a defined responsibility.

## 5. Semantic States

Morning Star shall distinguish the following semantic states.

### SS0 — Unoriented

The observer has encountered ORYNTH but lacks a reliable architectural model.

### SS1 — Partially Oriented

The observer recognizes portions of the ecosystem but retains unresolved structural or terminological gaps.

### SS2 — Provisionally Aligned

The observer's interpretation is substantially consistent with constitutional sources, but important dependencies or uncertainties remain unresolved.

### SS3 — Semantically Admissible

The interpretation preserves governed meaning sufficiently for the intended participation role.

### SS4 — Semantically Robust

The interpretation remains admissible across multiple representations, applications, questions, and cross-framework relationships.

### SS5 — Interpretation Drift

The interpretation has departed from one or more constitutional sources, dependencies, identities, or boundaries.

### SS6 — Semantic Fragmentation

The participant retains ORYNTH terminology while operating from a materially incompatible constitutional model.

### SS7 — Semantically Indeterminate

Available evidence is insufficient to classify the interpretation as admissible or inadmissible.

Semantic states shall be role-relative where the required depth of understanding differs by responsibility.

## 6. Interpretation Classes

Every governed interpretation shall be classified as one of the following.

### IC1 — Canonical Interpretation

Meaning stated directly by an authoritative constitutional source.

### IC2 — Constitutionally Equivalent Interpretation

A distinct representation preserving the same governed meaning.

### IC3 — Derived Interpretation

A traceable conclusion produced from one or more constitutional sources.

### IC4 — Provisional Interpretation

A traceable but unresolved interpretation awaiting additional evidence or review.

### IC5 — Bounded Interpretive Variation

A permissible difference that does not alter constitutional identity, dependency, authority, or operational consequence.

### IC6 — Inadmissible Interpretation

An interpretation that materially violates constitutional meaning, scope, identity, dependency, provenance, or authority.

### IC7 — Unclassifiable Interpretation

An interpretation for which available evidence is insufficient.

No interpretation shall be classified as inadmissible solely because it uses different language, representation, analogy, or learning sequence.

## 8. Observer-State Assessment
Observer-state assessment shall determine:

prior exposure to ORYNTH;
intended participation role;
known frameworks and applications;
imported terminology or external conceptual assumptions;
missing foundational dependencies;
unresolved uncertainty;
signs of interpretation drift;
provenance of current understanding.

Observer-state assessment shall not infer competence from credentials, status, institutional affiliation, vocabulary, or confidence alone.

Assessment shall rely on reconstructable evidence of interpretation and application.

## 9. Dependency Governance
Morning Star shall maintain a canonical dependency graph containing:

foundational dependencies;
conditional dependencies;
role-specific dependencies;
cross-framework dependencies;
prohibited dependency inversions;
optional enrichment pathways.

The dependency graph shall be a partial order where constitutional logic permits multiple valid learning sequences.

A dependency shall be mandatory only where its absence materially prevents semantic admissibility for the intended role.

Dependency violations shall trigger reconstruction rather than automatic exclusion.

## 10. Framework Identity Preservation
Each ORYNTH framework shall maintain an identity record specifying:

canonical name;
constitutional subject;
governance domain;
primary invariants;
authoritative source artifacts;
scope;
exclusions;
dependencies;
derived implementations;
relationships to adjacent frameworks;
conditions of constitutional continuity;
prohibited false equivalences.

Morning Star shall not infer framework identity solely from implementation, vocabulary, branding, domain use, or conceptual resemblance.

## 11. Traceability Requirements
Every derived or provisional interpretation shall preserve:

source artifact;
source version or date where available;
relevant source location;
derivation pathway;
interpretive transformations;
uncertainty status;
responsible interpreter or generating process where applicable;
relationship to superseded interpretations.

Where multiple sources contribute, each source and its distinct authority shall remain identifiable.

Summaries and educational artifacts shall not silently replace their constitutional sources.

## 12. Uncertainty Governance
Morning Star shall preserve semantic uncertainty explicitly.

Permitted uncertainty states shall include:

unresolved terminology;
incomplete dependency knowledge;
conflicting constitutional sources;
provisional framework relationships;
unavailable evidence;
ambiguous representation;
pending constitutional revision.

Uncertainty shall not be converted into confidence, rejection, or constitutional admission without supporting evidence.

Unknown shall remain distinct from false, inadmissible, incomplete, and contradictory.

## 13. Drift Detection
Interpretation drift may be indicated by:

untraceable constitutional claims;
terminology used outside its governed meaning;
framework responsibilities assigned incorrectly;
dependency inversion;
conflation of theory, protocol, runtime, audit, narrative, and implementation;
derived artifacts treated as superseding constitutional sources;
unexplained changes in interpretation across time;
disagreement producing materially different constitutional or operational consequences.

Drift detection shall evaluate consequences and constitutional relationships, not wording differences alone.

## 14. Semantic Reconstruction
When drift or fragmentation is detected, Morning Star shall support reconstruction through:

Current Interpretation Capture
        ↓
Source and Provenance Review
        ↓
Dependency Gap Identification
        ↓
Framework Identity Reconciliation
        ↓
Terminology and Ontology Alignment
        ↓
Uncertainty Preservation
        ↓
Reconstructed Interpretation
        ↓
Role-Specific Admissibility Review

Reconstruction shall preserve the prior interpretation as evidence rather than erase it.

## 15. Admissible Interpretive Diversity
Morning Star shall permit multiple representations where constitutional identity remains preserved.

Admissible diversity may include:

alternative wording;
visual representation;
mathematical representation;
narrative representation;
computational representation;
domain-specific analogy;
different valid learning paths;
distinct explanatory depth appropriate to role.

Interpretive diversity becomes inadmissible when it materially alters:

constitutional identity;
authority;
dependency;
scope;
invariant;
evidentiary status;
operational consequence.
## 16. Role-Bounded Admissibility
Semantic admissibility shall be evaluated relative to intended responsibility.

An observer need not possess the same constitutional depth as an implementer.

An implementer need not possess the same governance authority as a constitutional steward.

No participant shall exercise a role whose constitutional requirements exceed their demonstrated semantic admissibility and granted authority.

Role-bounded admissibility shall not prevent continued learning, contribution, disagreement, or provisional exploration.

## 17. Stewardship Governance
Constitutional stewardship shall require demonstrated competence in:

canonical interpretation;
framework identity preservation;
dependency reasoning;
provenance and evidence reconstruction;
uncertainty governance;
revision distinction;
conflict analysis;
succession continuity;
constitutional restraint.

Understanding alone shall not grant authority.

Stewardship authority shall be explicit, bounded, reviewable, transferable through governed procedure, and revocable where continuity or integrity is threatened.

## 18. Constitutional Revision Propagation
When a constitutional source changes, Morning Star shall identify:

affected terminology;
affected framework relationships;
affected dependency paths;
affected interpretive artifacts;
affected role requirements;
prior interpretations requiring review;
unchanged constitutional invariants.

A revision shall not be propagated as though it had always existed.

Historical interpretation, prior authority, and superseded meaning shall remain reconstructable.

## 19. Future Framework Admission
A future ORYNTH framework shall not enter the canonical ecosystem map until it has:

a defined constitutional subject;
a distinct governance domain;
explicit scope and exclusions;
identifiable invariants;
authoritative source artifacts;
specified dependencies;
declared relationships to existing frameworks;
evidence that it does not merely duplicate or rename existing architecture;
a governed constitutional status.

Morning Star shall represent provisional projects as provisional rather than prematurely canonical.

## 20. Relationship to Integrity Preservation Theory
Morning Star shall treat semantic interpretation as a bounded object potentially subject to integrity preservation analysis.

The relationship to IPT remains provisionally classified as one of the following:

direct IPT instantiation;
domain-specific IPT application;
shared constitutional pattern;
independent but interoperable architecture.

Morning Star shall not duplicate IPT's general theory of integrity.

The canonical model and verification phase shall determine the admissible relationship.

## 22. Specification Invariants
Morning Star shall preserve the following invariants:

MSI-1 — Source Supremacy

A derived interpretation shall not silently supersede its constitutional source.

MSI-2 — Identity Preservation

Interpretation shall not materially alter framework identity.

MSI-3 — Dependency Preservation

Required constitutional dependencies shall remain reconstructable.

MSI-4 — Uncertainty Preservation

Unresolved meaning shall remain explicitly unresolved.

MSI-5 — Role Boundedness

Semantic admissibility shall be evaluated according to intended responsibility.

MSI-6 — Traceable Derivation

Every noncanonical interpretation shall preserve a reconstructable derivation pathway.

MSI-7 — Representation Independence

Equivalent constitutional meaning may survive across different representational forms.

MSI-8 — Revision Visibility

Constitutional change shall remain distinguishable from prior canonical meaning.

MSI-9 — Stewardship Separation

Understanding, contribution, representation, and constitutional authority shall remain distinct.

MSI-10 — Nonreplacement

Morning Star shall govern interpretation without replacing the constitutional frameworks being interpreted.

## 23. Prohibited Conflations
Morning Star shall prohibit the following conflations:

interpretation with authority;
familiarity with competence;
confidence with admissibility;
summary with constitutional source;
terminology agreement with conceptual alignment;
implementation with framework identity;
contribution with stewardship;
narrative representation with literal constitutional specification;
conceptual similarity with constitutional equivalence;
uncertainty with error;
disagreement with inadmissibility;
revision with continuity;
entry architecture with general education.
