## Constitutional Subject

Morning Star studies the conditions under which an observer, participant, implementer, or steward can enter a complex constitutional ecosystem without introducing interpretation drift, semantic fragmentation, false equivalence, or unauthorized reinterpretation.

Its constitutional subject is the preservation of semantic integrity across the transition from first contact with ORYNTH to competent participation within its scientific, technical, narrative, assurance, and governance architectures.

The research concerns not merely whether information is available, but whether the architecture of entry preserves:

- canonical meaning;
- framework identity;
- dependency order;
- distinctions between theory, protocol, runtime, audit, narrative substrate, and application;
- traceability from derived interpretation to constitutional source;
- explicit uncertainty where interpretation is incomplete;
- resistance to semantic drift across people, documents, implementations, and time.

Morning Star therefore treats entry as a governed constitutional process rather than informal onboarding, documentation consumption, or unstructured education.

## Research Question

What constitutional architecture is necessary to preserve canonical interpretation from an observer's first encounter with ORYNTH through full participation in its scientific, technical, assurance, narrative, and governance systems, while preventing interpretation drift, semantic fragmentation, unauthorized reinterpretation, and loss of framework identity?

## Research Objective

Determine and formally specify the constitutional architecture required to preserve semantic integrity throughout the lifecycle of participation in the ORYNTH ecosystem.

The research shall identify the constitutional mechanisms necessary to:

- establish admissible entry into the ecosystem;
- preserve canonical interpretation across all constitutional artifacts;
- define dependency order for concepts, frameworks, and applications;
- prevent interpretation drift during learning, implementation, extension, and governance;
- distinguish constitutional knowledge from derived implementations;
- support reproducible understanding across independent observers;
- provide a canonical progression from initial orientation to constitutional stewardship.

The resulting specification shall serve as the constitutional basis from which the Morning Star governance corpus is derived.

## Falsifiability

The constitutional hypotheses of Morning Star shall be considered falsified, wholly or in part, if one or more of the following conditions are demonstrated:

### F1 — Semantic Non-Convergence

Independent observers following the complete Morning Star constitutional entry architecture consistently derive materially different constitutional interpretations.

### F2 — Dependency Failure

The prescribed constitutional dependency order can be altered without affecting the admissibility or correctness of constitutional interpretation.

### F3 — Drift Persistence

### F4 — Architectural Insufficiency

The constitutional architecture cannot accommodate newly developed ORYNTH frameworks without requiring fundamental reinterpretation of previously established constitutional artifacts.

### F5 — Loss of Traceability

Derived constitutional interpretations cannot be reconstructed to their originating constitutional sources.

Any confirmed falsification shall require constitutional review, revision, or replacement of the affected Morning Star constitutional artifacts.
