# Change Control Governance

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the governance framework for controlled changes to the ORYNTH / Morning Star certified architecture.

---

## Governance Principle

Change Control Governance ensures architectural evolution occurs through validated transitions rather than uncontrolled modification.

Every change must preserve constitutional identity, dependency relationships, and architectural continuity.

---

## Change Control Domains

Includes:

- change proposal;
- impact assessment;
- dependency review;
- semantic review;
- constitutional validation;
- approval authority;
- transition recording.

---

## Change Control Lifecycle


PROPOSED CHANGE

↓

CHANGE CLASSIFICATION

↓

IMPACT ANALYSIS

↓

DEPENDENCY REVIEW

↓

CONSTITUTIONAL REVIEW

↓

VALIDATION

↓

APPROVED TRANSITION

↓

ARCHITECTURE UPDATE

↓

HISTORICAL RECORD


---

## Change Constraints

A change must preserve:

- architecture identity;
- purpose;
- semantic boundaries;
- dependency order;
- governance authority;
- provenance;
- continuity.

---

## Principle

Change Control Governance enables controlled evolution while maintaining the integrity of the certified reference architecture.
