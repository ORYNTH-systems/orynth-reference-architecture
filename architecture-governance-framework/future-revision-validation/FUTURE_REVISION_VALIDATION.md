# Future Revision Validation

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the validation framework for future revisions of the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Validation Principle

Future revisions must demonstrate that architectural evolution preserves identity, purpose, dependencies, semantics, governance, and continuity.

A revision is accepted only when it remains within constitutional boundaries.

---

## Validation Domains

Includes:

- revision purpose;
- affected architecture layers;
- dependency impact;
- semantic impact;
- identity impact;
- governance compatibility;
- continuity preservation;
- release readiness.

---

## Revision Validation Lifecycle


PROPOSED REVISION

↓

REVISION CLASSIFICATION

↓

ARCHITECTURAL IMPACT REVIEW

↓

DEPENDENCY VALIDATION

↓

CONSTITUTIONAL COMPATIBILITY CHECK

↓

IMPLEMENTATION REVIEW

↓

CERTIFICATION DECISION

↓

REFERENCE STATE UPDATE


---

## Validation Outcomes

Possible states:

- VALIDATED;
- VALIDATED WITH CONDITIONS;
- REQUIRES REVISION;
- REJECTED.

---

## Principle

Future Revision Validation ensures Morning Star evolves through governed improvement while preserving architectural continuity.
