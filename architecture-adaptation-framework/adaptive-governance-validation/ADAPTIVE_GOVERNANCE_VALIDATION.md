# Adaptive Governance Validation

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the governance validation framework for intentional architectural adaptation.

---

## Governance Principle

Adaptation becomes part of the architecture only when it has passed defined governance review, constraint evaluation, evidence validation, and continuity assessment.

---

## Governance Validation Domains

Includes:

- adaptation authorization;
- constraint compliance;
- evidence sufficiency;
- impact assessment;
- continuity preservation;
- implementation readiness.

---

## Validation Lifecycle


ADAPTIVE STATE PROPOSED

↓

GOVERNANCE REVIEW

↓

CONSTRAINT VERIFICATION

↓

EVIDENCE REVIEW

↓

APPROVAL DECISION

↓

IMPLEMENTATION

↓

POST-ADAPTATION VALIDATION


---

## Governance Requirements

Adaptive changes must preserve:

- architectural identity;
- constitutional boundaries;
- semantic integrity;
- dependency order;
- evidence lineage;
- continuity.

---

## Principle

Adaptive Governance Validation ensures evolution remains a controlled architectural process.
