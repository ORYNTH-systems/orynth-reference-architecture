# Long-Term Evolution Continuity

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the continuity model governing long-term architectural evolution within the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Evolution Continuity Principle

Evolution remains valid when future architecture states preserve traceable relationships to their validated origins.

Change alone does not establish continuity. Continuity requires preserved identity, evidence, governance, and historical connection.

---

## Continuity Domains

Includes:

- identity continuity;
- semantic continuity;
- governance continuity;
- evidence continuity;
- version continuity;
- evolutionary continuity.

---

## Evolution Continuity Lifecycle


CERTIFIED REFERENCE STATE

↓

ADAPTIVE TRANSITION

↓

GOVERNANCE VALIDATION

↓

IMPLEMENTED EVOLUTION

↓

STATE COMPARISON

↓

CONTINUITY CONFIRMATION

↓

NEW REFERENCE STATE


---

## Continuity Requirements

Long-term evolution must preserve:

- original architectural purpose;
- constitutional boundaries;
- dependency relationships;
- evidence lineage;
- validation history;
- adaptation rationale.

---

## Principle

Long-Term Evolution Continuity allows Morning Star to evolve indefinitely without losing its architectural identity.
