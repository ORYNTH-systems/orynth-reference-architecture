# Self-Regulating Architecture Controls

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the control framework enabling autonomous regulation of the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Regulation Principle

Self-Regulating Architecture Controls maintain architectural stability by continuously evaluating conditions and applying governed responses.

Regulation occurs within established constraints and does not replace constitutional governance.

---

## Control Domains

Includes:

- state stabilization;
- integrity preservation;
- constraint enforcement;
- compliance monitoring;
- transition control;
- recovery coordination.

---

## Control Lifecycle


ARCHITECTURE CONDITION OBSERVED

↓

CONTROL REQUIREMENT IDENTIFIED

↓

RESPONSE RULE EVALUATED

↓

CONTROL ACTION EXECUTED

↓

RESULT VERIFIED

↓

CONTROL HISTORY PRESERVED


---

## Control Requirements

Self-regulation must preserve:

- architecture identity;
- governance boundaries;
- evidence lineage;
- validation requirements;
- continuity relationships.

---

## Principle

Self-Regulating Architecture Controls provide autonomous stability while preserving governed evolution.
