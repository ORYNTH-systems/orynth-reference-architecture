# Autonomous Validation Intelligence

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the autonomous validation framework for the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Validation Principle

Autonomous Validation Intelligence continuously evaluates architecture conditions against established requirements, evidence, and governance constraints.

Validation confirms whether a state remains acceptable before autonomous continuation.

---

## Validation Domains

Includes:

- integrity validation;
- governance validation;
- evidence validation;
- resilience validation;
- adaptation validation;
- continuity validation.

---

## Validation Lifecycle


ARCHITECTURE STATE OBSERVED

↓

VALIDATION REQUIREMENTS IDENTIFIED

↓

EVIDENCE EVALUATED

↓

CONDITION VERIFIED

↓

VALIDATION DECISION GENERATED

↓

RESULT PRESERVED


---

## Validation Requirements

Autonomous validation must preserve:

- explainability;
- evidence lineage;
- decision traceability;
- governance alignment;
- continuity awareness.

---

## Principle

Autonomous Validation Intelligence provides continuous assurance that autonomous operations remain within defined architectural boundaries.
