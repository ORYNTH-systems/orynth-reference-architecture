# Autonomous Continuity Preservation

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the autonomous continuity preservation framework for the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Continuity Principle

Autonomous operation must preserve the relationship between previous validated states, current operating states, and future architectural states.

Autonomy without continuity creates uncontrolled divergence.

---

## Preservation Domains

Includes:

- identity preservation;
- semantic preservation;
- evidence preservation;
- governance preservation;
- state lineage preservation;
- historical preservation.

---

## Continuity Preservation Lifecycle


CURRENT ARCHITECTURE STATE

↓

AUTONOMOUS OPERATION

↓

STATE CHANGE DETECTION

↓

LINEAGE VALIDATION

↓

CONTINUITY CONFIRMATION

↓

REFERENCE STATE UPDATE

↓

PRESERVED ARCHITECTURAL MEMORY


---

## Preservation Requirements

Autonomous continuity must maintain:

- origin relationship;
- version history;
- validation records;
- governance decisions;
- evidence chains;
- transition records.

---

## Principle

Autonomous Continuity Preservation allows Morning Star to operate autonomously while remaining connected to its validated architectural lineage.
