# Resilience Continuity Validation

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the validation framework for confirming resilience continuity within the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Continuity Principle

A resilient architecture maintains its identity and essential relationships through disruption, recovery, and adaptation.

Continuity validation determines whether a recovered or evolved state remains connected to its validated origin.

---

## Validation Domains

Includes:

- identity continuity;
- functional continuity;
- governance continuity;
- evidence continuity;
- semantic continuity;
- version continuity.

---

## Continuity Validation Lifecycle


INITIAL RESILIENCE STATE

↓

DISRUPTION EVENT

↓

RECOVERY OR ADAPTATION

↓

STATE COMPARISON

↓

CONTINUITY EVALUATION

↓

VALIDATED RESILIENT STATE

↓

HISTORICAL PRESERVATION


---

## Continuity Requirements

The architecture must preserve:

- defining identity;
- critical dependencies;
- governance boundaries;
- evidence lineage;
- historical relationships.

---

## Principle

Resilience Continuity Validation confirms that Morning Star can change without losing architectural coherence.
