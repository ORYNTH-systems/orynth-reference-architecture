# Resilience Baseline Modeling

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the resilience baseline for the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Resilience Principle

A resilience baseline establishes the known architectural condition required to preserve identity, function, governance, and continuity when exposed to disruption.

Resilience is not the absence of failure. It is the ability to maintain coherent operation through changing conditions.

---

## Resilience Domains

Includes:

- structural resilience;
- semantic resilience;
- governance resilience;
- evidence resilience;
- continuity resilience;
- adaptive resilience.

## Baseline Requirements

The resilience baseline preserves:

- architecture identity;
- essential functions;
- dependency relationships;
- governance controls;
- evidence lineage;
- recovery conditions.

---

## Principle

The resilience baseline provides the reference condition used to evaluate architectural survival and adaptation.
