# Assurance Continuity Model

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the continuity model that preserves assurance across the lifecycle of the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Continuity Principle

Assurance must persist across architectural transitions.

A future architecture state maintains assurance only when its relationship to previous validated states remains traceable.

---

## Continuity Domains

Includes:

- baseline continuity;
- evidence continuity;
- validation continuity;
- governance continuity;
- version continuity;
- historical continuity.

---

## Assurance Continuity Lifecycle


ASSURANCE BASELINE

↓

ARCHITECTURE TRANSITION

↓

EVIDENCE PRESERVATION

↓

VALIDATION COMPARISON

↓

RELIABILITY CONFIRMATION

↓

UPDATED ASSURANCE STATE

↓

CONTINUOUS LINEAGE


---

## Continuity Requirements

Assurance continuity preserves:

- reference identity;
- evidence lineage;
- validation history;
- architectural relationships;
- governance alignment;
- reliability records.

---

## Principle

The Assurance Continuity Model ensures Morning Star remains assured through controlled evolution.
