# Architecture Reliability Assessment

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the reliability assessment framework for the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Reliability Principle

Architecture reliability measures the ability of the system to preserve intended identity, function, governance, and continuity while operating through change.

Reliability is demonstrated through evidence, validation, and preserved architectural behavior.

---

## Reliability Domains

Includes:

- structural reliability;
- semantic reliability;
- governance reliability;
- evidence reliability;
- continuity reliability;
- evolution reliability.

---

## Reliability Assessment Lifecycle


DEFINE RELIABILITY CONDITIONS

↓

COLLECT ASSURANCE EVIDENCE

↓

EVALUATE ARCHITECTURE STATE

↓

MEASURE RELIABILITY CONDITIONS

↓

IDENTIFY RISKS OR WEAKNESSES

↓

RECORD ASSESSMENT RESULT

↓

MAINTAIN RELIABILITY HISTORY


---

## Reliability Requirements

Architecture reliability requires preservation of:

- identity;
- dependencies;
- semantics;
- governance;
- evidence lineage;
- continuity.

---

## Principle

Architecture Reliability Assessment determines whether Morning Star remains dependable as a continuously evolving architecture.
