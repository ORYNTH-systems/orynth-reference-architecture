# Constitutional Reasoning Governance

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the governance framework for maintaining constitutional alignment during architectural reasoning processes.

---

## Reasoning Governance Principle

Reasoning must expand understanding while preserving:

- architectural identity;
- evidence relationships;
- constitutional constraints;
- semantic integrity;
- decision boundaries.

Reasoning may generate possibilities, but governed processes determine admissible conclusions.

---

## Governance Domains

Includes:

- reasoning authority;
- inference boundaries;
- evidence requirements;
- interpretation controls;
- decision validation;
- conclusion integration.

---

## Reasoning Governance Lifecycle


REASONING STATE CREATED

↓

CONTEXT AND CONSTRAINTS REVIEWED

↓

INFERENCE EVALUATED

↓

GOVERNANCE CONDITIONS APPLIED

↓

CONCLUSION VALIDATED

↓

REASONING STATE INTEGRATED


---

## Governance Requirements

Constitutional reasoning must preserve:

- source knowledge;
- inference pathway;
- assumptions;
- uncertainty;
- validation requirements;
- architectural boundaries.

---

## Principle

Constitutional Reasoning Governance ensures intelligence remains bounded by integrity, evidence, and architectural purpose.
