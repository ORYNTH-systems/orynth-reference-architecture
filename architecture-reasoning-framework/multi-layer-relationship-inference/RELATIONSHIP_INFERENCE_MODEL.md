# Relationship Inference Model

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the structural model for inferring relationships between architectural states.

---

## Relationship Principle

A relationship becomes meaningful when context, evidence, and dependencies demonstrate interaction between states.

---

## Relationship Inference Model


ARCHITECTURAL OBJECTS

RELATIONSHIP DEFINITIONS

CONTEXTUAL CONDITIONS

DEPENDENCY ANALYSIS

EVIDENCE

↓

INFERRED RELATIONSHIP

↓

REASONING OUTPUT


---

## Relationship Categories

### Structural Relationship

Identifies architectural organization.

### Dependency Relationship

Identifies required conditions between states.

### Temporal Relationship

Identifies evolution across time.

### Semantic Relationship

Identifies meaning connections.

### Causal Relationship

Identifies influence pathways.

---

## Inference Factors

Includes:

- relationship strength;
- evidence support;
- contextual validity;
- dependency integrity;
- uncertainty assessment.

---

## Principle

The Relationship Inference Model ensures conclusions emerge from connected architectural relationships rather than isolated observations.
