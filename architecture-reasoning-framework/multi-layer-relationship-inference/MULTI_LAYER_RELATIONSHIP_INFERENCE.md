# Multi-Layer Relationship Inference

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework for reasoning across interconnected ORYNTH / Morning Star architectural layers.

---

## Inference Principle

Complex architectural reasoning requires evaluating relationships across multiple states rather than isolated components.

Multi-layer inference considers:

- structural relationships;
- temporal relationships;
- dependency relationships;
- knowledge relationships;
- governance relationships.

---

## Inference Domains

Includes:

- framework-to-framework relationships;
- state transitions;
- evidence relationships;
- capability dependencies;
- evolutionary pathways.

---

## Inference Lifecycle


ARCHITECTURAL STATES IDENTIFIED

↓

RELATIONSHIPS MAPPED

↓

CROSS-LAYER CONDITIONS ANALYZED

↓

INFERENCE GENERATED

↓

REASONING VALIDATED

↓

RELATIONSHIP KNOWLEDGE UPDATED


---

## Inference Requirements

Multi-layer reasoning must preserve:

- relationship context;
- dependency order;
- source states;
- evidence lineage;
- uncertainty boundaries.

---

## Principle

Multi-Layer Relationship Inference enables architectural reasoning across the complete system without collapsing distinct layers into one another.
