# Reasoning Continuity Preservation

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework for preserving reasoning lineage across future ORYNTH / Morning Star architectural states.

---

## Continuity Principle

A reasoning outcome remains meaningful when its relationship to inputs, context, inference, governance, and validation is preserved.

Reasoning continuity maintains:

- source knowledge;
- reasoning pathway;
- inference relationships;
- governance history;
- validation conditions;
- conclusion lineage.

---

## Continuity Domains

Includes:

- reasoning history;
- inference lineage;
- decision continuity;
- knowledge relationships;
- architectural evolution.

---

## Continuity Requirements

Reasoning continuity must preserve:

- original inputs;
- contextual assumptions;
- relationship mappings;
- validation records;
- governance decisions;
- revision history.

---

## Principle

Reasoning Continuity Preservation ensures future architectural reasoning remains connected to the reasoning processes that produced previous understanding.
