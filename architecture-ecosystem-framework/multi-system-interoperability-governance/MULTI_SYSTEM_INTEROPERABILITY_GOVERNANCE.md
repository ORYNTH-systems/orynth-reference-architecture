# Multi-System Interoperability Governance

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the governance framework for interoperability between ORYNTH / Morning Star and external systems, architectures, and ecosystems.

---

## Interoperability Principle

Interoperability enables systems to communicate, cooperate, and exchange value while preserving the identity and governance requirements of each participating system.

Interoperability does not require architectural convergence.

---

## Interoperability Domains

Includes:

- data relationships;
- conceptual translation;
- technical interfaces;
- operational coordination;
- governance alignment;
- evidence exchange.

---

## Interoperability Lifecycle


SYSTEM RELATIONSHIP IDENTIFIED

↓

INTERFACE REQUIREMENTS DEFINED

↓

GOVERNANCE CONDITIONS ESTABLISHED

↓

INTERACTION VALIDATED

↓

INTEROPERABILITY ENABLED

↓

RELATIONSHIP MAINTAINED


---

## Governance Requirements

Interoperability must preserve:

- system identity;
- authority boundaries;
- semantic meaning;
- security conditions;
- evidence traceability;
- relationship history.

---

## Principle

Multi-System Interoperability Governance enables cooperation between architectures without loss of independent integrity.
