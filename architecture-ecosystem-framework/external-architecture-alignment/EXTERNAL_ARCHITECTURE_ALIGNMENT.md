# External Architecture Alignment

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework for aligning ORYNTH / Morning Star with external architectures, standards, systems, and research domains.

---

## Alignment Principle

External alignment establishes compatibility and relationship without requiring loss of architectural identity.

Alignment identifies where systems can cooperate, translate, or exchange knowledge while preserving independent boundaries.

---

## Alignment Domains

Includes:

- external frameworks;
- technical standards;
- research architectures;
- institutional systems;
- interoperability requirements;
- conceptual relationships.

---

## Alignment Lifecycle


EXTERNAL SYSTEM IDENTIFIED

↓

ARCHITECTURE COMPARISON

↓

RELATIONSHIP ANALYSIS

↓

COMPATIBILITY ASSESSMENT

↓

ALIGNMENT DECISION

↓

RELATIONSHIP RECORDED


---

## Alignment Requirements

External relationships must preserve:

- ORYNTH / Morning Star identity;
- constitutional principles;
- semantic integrity;
- governance boundaries;
- evidence traceability.

---

## Principle

External Architecture Alignment enables ecosystem participation without architectural assimilation.
