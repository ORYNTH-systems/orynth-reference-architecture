# Embodiment Control Model

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define controls governing architectural embodiment.

---

## Control Principle

Embodiment controls maintain alignment between identity, presence, interaction, and continuity.

---

## Control Model


ARCHITECTURAL IDENTITY

EMBODIMENT STATE

OPERATIONAL PRESENCE

ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS

GOVERNANCE RULES

↓

CONTROLLED EMBODIMENT STATE

↓

PERSISTENT OPERATIONAL PRESENCE


---

## Control Categories

### Identity Control

Maintains continuity of architectural identity.

### Presence Control

Manages operational existence.

### Interaction Control

Defines permitted environmental relationships.

### Transition Control

Governs changes between embodiment states.

### Continuity Control

Preserves historical lineage.

---

## Control Factors

Includes:

- identity integrity;
- operational consistency;
- environmental compatibility;
- governance alignment;
- continuity preservation.

---

## Principle

The Embodiment Control Model prevents operational presence from separating from its governing architecture.
