# Embodiment State Modeling

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework for representing embodiment states within ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Embodiment Principle

Embodiment represents the transition from internal architectural capability into persistent operational presence.

An embodiment state preserves:

- architectural identity;
- operational context;
- agency capability;
- environmental relationship;
- continuity conditions.

---

## Embodiment Domains

Includes:

- operational presence;
- identity representation;
- environmental interaction;
- persistent state;
- capability manifestation;
- system visibility.

---

## Embodiment Lifecycle


ARCHITECTURAL IDENTITY ESTABLISHED

↓

AGENCY CAPABILITY AVAILABLE

↓

EMBODIMENT CONDITIONS EVALUATED

↓

OPERATIONAL PRESENCE FORMED

↓

ENVIRONMENTAL INTERACTION ENABLED

↓

EMBODIMENT STATE PRESERVED


---

## Embodiment Requirements

Embodiment states must preserve:

- originating cognitive state;
- agency lineage;
- operational purpose;
- governance conditions;
- environmental context;
- historical continuity.

---

## Principle

Embodiment State Modeling ensures operational presence remains connected to the architecture that produced it.
