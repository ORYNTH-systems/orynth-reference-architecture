# Publication Policy

Document: PUBLICATION_POLICY.md

Classification: Constitutional Governance

Status: Draft

Version: 0.1.0

---

## Purpose

The Publication Policy governs the constitutional release, distribution, archival preservation, and public representation of Morning Star artifacts.

Publication communicates constitutional artifacts.

Publication does not itself establish constitutional authority.

---

## Constitutional Principles

Every publication shall be:

- traceable;
- versioned;
- reconstructable;
- historically preserved;
- provenance-backed;
- constitutionally bounded;
- publicly verifiable.

---

## Publication Classes

### Working Draft

Internal development artifact.

No canonical authority.

### Review Draft

Submitted for constitutional review.

Not yet constitutionally admitted.

### Constitutional Release

An officially admitted constitutional publication corresponding to a governed version.

### Historical Archive

A preserved publication retained solely for constitutional continuity and historical reconstruction.

Archived publications remain citable but shall preserve their supersession status.

---

## Publication Record

Every publication shall preserve:

- Publication Identifier;
- version;
- publication class;
- publication date;
- governing artifacts;
- publication authority;
- revision references;
- supersession status;
- repository reference;
- integrity verification.

---

## Release Requirements

Before constitutional publication:

- required reviews shall be complete;
- governing artifacts shall be internally consistent;
- version shall be assigned;
- changelog shall be updated;
- constitutional references shall resolve correctly;
- publication authority shall be recorded.

---

## Public Representation

Public publications shall accurately represent the constitutional scope of Morning Star.

No publication shall imply:

- authority beyond its documented scope;
- supersession without record;
- canonical status for provisional material;
- constitutional revisions that have not been admitted.

---

## Archival Preservation

Every constitutional publication shall remain permanently preserved together with:

- publication metadata;
- release version;
- historical context;
- integrity verification;
- successor references where applicable.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Every constitutional publication shall preserve the identity, provenance, traceability, and historical continuity of Morning Star while accurately communicating its governed constitutional state.
