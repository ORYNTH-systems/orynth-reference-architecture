# Versioning Policy

Document: VERSIONING_POLICY.md

Classification: Constitutional Governance

Status: Draft

Version: 0.1.0

---

## Purpose

The Versioning Policy governs how constitutional versions are assigned, released, preserved, and referenced throughout the Morning Star project.

Version identifiers describe constitutional evolution.

They do not independently establish constitutional authority.

---

## Constitutional Principles

Versioning shall be:

- explicit;
- sequential;
- traceable;
- historically preserved;
- reconstructable;
- publicly referenceable.

---

## Version Structure

Morning Star uses the following version format:

MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH

Example:

0.1.0

Where:

### MAJOR

Represents a constitutionally admitted change affecting canonical architecture or constitutional identity.

### MINOR

Represents the admission of constitutionally significant additions that preserve constitutional identity.

### PATCH

Represents editorial, corrective, or non-semantic improvements that do not alter constitutional meaning.

---

## Initial Constitutional Baseline

Version:

0.1.0

Represents:

The first constitutionally complete Canonical Model of Morning Star.

---

## Version Assignment Rules

Every released version shall preserve:

- version identifier;
- release date;
- governing artifacts;
- revision summary;
- amendment references;
- superseded version;
- applicable governance records.

---

## Supersession

A new version shall never erase a previous version.

Every superseded version shall remain:

- archived;
- reconstructable;
- historically referenceable;
- linked to its successor.

---

## Publication

Every published version shall correspond to a permanent Git tag and an associated changelog entry.

Version identifiers shall remain immutable after publication.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Every constitutional version shall preserve the historical continuity, identity, and traceability of Morning Star while providing an immutable reference point for future constitutional evolution.
