# Authority Model

Classification: Constitutional Governance

Status: Draft

Version: 0.1.0

---

## Purpose

The Authority Model defines how constitutional authority is recognized, bounded, exercised, delegated, reviewed, and revoked within the Morning Star project.

Authority governs constitutional decisions.

Authority does not create constitutional truth.

---

## Constitutional Principles

Authority shall always be:

- explicit;
- bounded;
- reviewable;
- reconstructable;
- historically preserved;
- evidence-supported;
- constitutionally traceable.

---

## Sources of Authority

Constitutional authority may originate only from:

- admitted constitutional artifacts;
- constitutionally recognized stewardship;
- formally admitted amendments;
- explicit constitutional succession.

No individual, institution, implementation, publication, or software system acquires constitutional authority merely through existence, popularity, expertise, possession, or usage.

---

## Authority Classes

### Constitutional Authority

May admit or revise canonical constitutional meaning.

### Stewardship Authority

May preserve constitutional continuity without independently redefining constitutional meaning.

### Review Authority

May evaluate constitutional proposals and produce recommendations.

### Publication Authority

May authorize official constitutional releases.

### Operational Authority

May manage project operations without modifying constitutional architecture.

---

## Authority Constraints

Authority shall never be used to:

- conceal uncertainty;
- erase historical revisions;
- bypass amendment procedures;
- redefine constitutional scope without governed review;
- invalidate canonical provenance.

---

## Delegation

Delegated authority shall identify:

- delegating authority;
- recipient;
- scope;
- duration;
- limitations;
- review requirements;
- revocation conditions.

Delegated authority shall never exceed the authority possessed by the delegating authority.

---

## Revocation

Authority may be revoked only through constitutionally governed review.

Revocation shall preserve:

- reason;
- effective date;
- supporting evidence;
- historical record.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Every exercise of constitutional authority shall remain traceable, reviewable, reconstructable, and historically preserved.
