# Revision Protocol

Document: REVISION_PROTOCOL.md

Classification: Constitutional Governance

Status: Draft

Version: 0.1.0

---

## Purpose

The Revision Protocol governs how constitutional changes are proposed, evaluated, approved, implemented, documented, and preserved within Morning Star.

A revision changes constitutional artifacts.

A revision does not automatically change constitutional identity.

---

## Constitutional Revision Principles

Every revision shall be:

- explicit;
- evidence-supported;
- constitutionally bounded;
- historically preserved;
- reconstructable;
- reviewable;
- traceable.

---

## Revision Classes

### Editorial Revision

Corrects formatting, grammar, typography, or clarity without changing constitutional meaning.

### Structural Revision

Reorganizes constitutional presentation while preserving governing meaning.

### Semantic Revision

Clarifies constitutional meaning without changing constitutional identity.

### Constitutional Revision

Introduces an approved change affecting canonical governance, relationships, or constitutional behavior.

### Supersession

Replaces an earlier constitutional artifact while preserving complete historical traceability.

---

## Revision Record

Every revision shall preserve:

- Revision Identifier;
- affected artifact;
- revision class;
- author;
- review authority;
- rationale;
- evidence;
- effective date;
- superseded artifacts;
- propagation requirements.

---

## Required Review

Every proposed revision shall undergo constitutional review appropriate to its revision class before admission.

No revision shall become canonical solely through publication.

---

## Historical Preservation

Every superseded revision shall remain permanently preserved together with:

- revision history;
- rationale;
- effective period;
- successor reference.

Historical preservation is mandatory.

---

## Revision Propagation

Approved revisions shall identify every affected:

- governance document;
- constitutional artifact;
- derived artifact;
- terminology reference;
- dependency model;
- stewardship record.

Propagation shall be documented before constitutional closure.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Every constitutional revision shall preserve the continuity, traceability, and historical identity of Morning Star while documenting every material constitutional change.
