# Amendment Protocol

Document: AMENDMENT_PROTOCOL.md

Classification: Constitutional Governance

Status: Draft

Version: 0.1.0

---

## Purpose

The Amendment Protocol governs constitutional changes that alter the governing architecture, identity, scope, or canonical meaning of Morning Star.

An amendment is the highest form of constitutional change.

Every amendment shall preserve the complete constitutional history of the project.

---

## Constitutional Principles

Every amendment shall be:

- explicit;
- constitutionally justified;
- evidence-supported;
- historically preserved;
- reconstructable;
- reviewable;
- traceable;
- non-destructive.

---

## Amendment Classes

### Minor Amendment

Clarifies constitutional architecture without materially changing framework identity.

### Major Amendment

Changes constitutional architecture while preserving constitutional continuity.

### Foundational Amendment

Changes constitutional identity, governing principles, or constitutional scope.

Foundational Amendments require the highest level of constitutional review.

---

## Amendment Record

Every amendment shall preserve:

- Amendment Identifier;
- affected constitutional artifacts;
- amendment class;
- constitutional rationale;
- supporting evidence;
- admitting authority;
- effective date;
- superseded provisions;
- propagation requirements;
- historical references.

---

## Admission Requirements

Before admission, every amendment shall identify:

- constitutional necessity;
- affected invariants;
- compatibility with existing architecture;
- affected governance documents;
- required implementation updates;
- stewardship implications;
- unresolved uncertainty.

---

## Amendment Review

Every amendment shall undergo constitutional review before admission.

Review shall evaluate:

- constitutional consistency;
- identity preservation;
- framework continuity;
- dependency effects;
- governance impacts;
- historical preservation.

No amendment becomes canonical without constitutional admission.

---

## Historical Preservation

Every superseded constitutional provision shall remain permanently preserved together with:

- amendment identifier;
- effective period;
- successor provision;
- constitutional rationale.

Constitutional history shall never be destroyed.

---

## Constitutional Invariant

Every admitted amendment shall preserve the constitutional continuity, historical integrity, and reconstructability of Morning Star while explicitly documenting every constitutional change.
