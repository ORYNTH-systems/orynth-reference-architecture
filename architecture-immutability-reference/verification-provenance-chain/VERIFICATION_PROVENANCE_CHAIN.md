# Verification and Provenance Chain

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the verification and provenance framework used to establish traceability, validation evidence, and historical lineage for ORYNTH immutable architecture states.

---

## Provenance Principle

A provenance chain preserves the complete relationship between an architectural state, its origin, validation evidence, transformation history, and current reference condition.

Verification establishes trust through traceable lineage.

---

## Provenance Domains

Includes:

- architecture origin;
- state identity;
- evidence records;
- validation results;
- transformation history;
- dependency relationships;
- authorization records;
- reconstruction pathways.

---

## Verification Lifecycle


IDENTIFY REFERENCE STATE

↓

COLLECT SUPPORTING EVIDENCE

↓

VERIFY ARCHITECTURAL CONDITION

↓

LINK PROVENANCE RECORDS

↓

VALIDATE TRACEABILITY

↓

PRESERVE VERIFICATION CHAIN

↓

ENABLE FUTURE RECONSTRUCTION


---

## Principle

Verification and provenance chains ensure ORYNTH immutable states remain identifiable, auditable, and reconstructable across future architecture cycles.
