# Contextual Architecture Reasoning

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the reasoning framework used to interpret intelligence states within architectural context.

---

## Reasoning Principle

Intelligence becomes meaningful through relationships, constraints, history, dependencies, and operational context.

Reasoning does not replace evidence. It organizes relationships between known states and possible conclusions.

---

## Reasoning Domains

Includes:

- contextual relationships;
- dependency analysis;
- temporal reasoning;
- causal relationships;
- architectural alignment;
- uncertainty evaluation.

---

## Reasoning Lifecycle


INTELLIGENCE STATE

↓

CONTEXT IDENTIFICATION

↓

RELATIONSHIP ANALYSIS

↓

REASONING GENERATION

↓

VALIDATION REVIEW

↓

GOVERNED CONCLUSION


---

## Reasoning Requirements

Contextual reasoning must preserve:

- source integrity;
- uncertainty boundaries;
- dependency relationships;
- evidence traceability;
- governance constraints.

---

## Principle

Contextual Architecture Reasoning transforms intelligence states into structured understanding while preserving architectural integrity.
