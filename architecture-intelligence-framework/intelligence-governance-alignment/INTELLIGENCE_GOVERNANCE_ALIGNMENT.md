# Intelligence Governance Alignment

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the governance alignment framework for intelligence operations within the ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Governance Principle

Intelligence must operate within defined architectural boundaries.

Intelligence generation, interpretation, and application require alignment with authority, evidence, constraints, and continuity requirements.

---

## Governance Domains

Includes:

- intelligence authority;
- evidence requirements;
- uncertainty governance;
- decision boundaries;
- policy alignment;
- continuity requirements.

---

## Governance Alignment Lifecycle


INTELLIGENCE STATE

↓

GOVERNANCE EVALUATION

↓

POLICY ALIGNMENT

↓

AUTHORITY VERIFICATION

↓

APPLICATION ELIGIBILITY

↓

GOVERNED INTELLIGENCE OUTPUT


---

## Alignment Requirements

Intelligence operations must preserve:

- source integrity;
- evidence lineage;
- uncertainty visibility;
- decision accountability;
- constitutional boundaries.

---

## Principle

Intelligence Governance Alignment ensures intelligence remains a governed architectural capability rather than an uncontrolled inference process.
