# Performance Optimization Model

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework for improving ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture performance through evidence-based analysis and controlled refinement.

---

## Optimization Principle

Optimization improves system capability while preserving:

- architectural identity;
- governance constraints;
- validated properties;
- historical continuity.

Optimization is controlled improvement, not uncontrolled alteration.

---

## Performance Domains

Includes:

- operational efficiency;
- execution reliability;
- resource utilization;
- governance effectiveness;
- validation effectiveness;
- architectural responsiveness.

---

## Optimization Lifecycle


CURRENT ARCHITECTURE STATE

↓

PERFORMANCE OBSERVATION

↓

IMPROVEMENT OPPORTUNITY IDENTIFIED

↓

OPTIMIZATION PROPOSED

↓

CHANGE VALIDATED

↓

IMPROVED STATE ESTABLISHED


---

## Optimization Requirements

Performance optimization must preserve:

- baseline conditions;
- measurement methodology;
- evidence lineage;
- change rationale;
- validation requirements.

---

## Principle

The Performance Optimization Model ensures improvement occurs through measurable and governed refinement.
