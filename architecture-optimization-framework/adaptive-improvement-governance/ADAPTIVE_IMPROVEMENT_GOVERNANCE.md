# Adaptive Improvement Governance

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the governance framework for adaptive improvement of ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture.

---

## Improvement Principle

Adaptive improvement enables architecture evolution through controlled learning while preserving:

- constitutional identity;
- validated capabilities;
- governance boundaries;
- historical continuity.

Improvement must be responsive without becoming uncontrolled change.

---

## Governance Domains

Includes:

- improvement authorization;
- change evaluation;
- evidence review;
- compatibility assessment;
- validation oversight;
- improvement lifecycle management.

---

## Improvement Governance Lifecycle


IMPROVEMENT OPPORTUNITY IDENTIFIED

↓

EVIDENCE REVIEWED

↓

IMPROVEMENT PROPOSED

↓

GOVERNANCE EVALUATION

↓

CHANGE AUTHORIZED

↓

IMPROVEMENT VALIDATED

↓

UPDATED REFERENCE STATE


---

## Governance Requirements

Adaptive improvement must preserve:

- evidence lineage;
- decision rationale;
- change boundaries;
- validation requirements;
- reference architecture continuity.

---

## Principle

Adaptive Improvement Governance ensures the architecture can improve while remaining coherent and trustworthy.
