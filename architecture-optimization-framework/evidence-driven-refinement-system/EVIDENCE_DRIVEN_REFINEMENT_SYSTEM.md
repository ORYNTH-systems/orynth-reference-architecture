# Evidence-Driven Refinement System

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the system for improving ORYNTH / Morning Star architecture through validated evidence, observed performance, and accumulated knowledge.

---

## Refinement Principle

Refinement occurs when evidence demonstrates an opportunity to improve architecture capability while preserving established integrity.

Refinement requires:

- evidence;
- analysis;
- justification;
- validation;
- recorded transition.

---

## Refinement Domains

Includes:

- performance findings;
- validation results;
- operational observations;
- stakeholder feedback;
- impact measurements;
- continuity analysis.

---

## Refinement Lifecycle


EVIDENCE OBSERVED

↓

IMPROVEMENT OPPORTUNITY IDENTIFIED

↓

REFINEMENT PROPOSED

↓

GOVERNANCE REVIEW

↓

CHANGE VALIDATED

↓

REFINED ARCHITECTURE STATE


---

## Refinement Requirements

Evidence-driven refinement must preserve:

- historical lineage;
- architectural identity;
- decision rationale;
- validation records;
- uncertainty conditions.

---

## Principle

The Evidence-Driven Refinement System converts accumulated knowledge into controlled architectural improvement.
