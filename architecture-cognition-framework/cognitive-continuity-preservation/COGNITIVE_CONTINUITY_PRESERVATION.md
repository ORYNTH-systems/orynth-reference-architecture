# Cognitive Continuity Preservation

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the framework for preserving cognitive lineage across future ORYNTH / Morning Star architectural states.

---

## Continuity Principle

A cognitive state remains meaningful when its relationship to knowledge, discovery, reasoning, synthesis, governance, and action is preserved.

Cognitive continuity maintains:

- source knowledge;
- discovery pathways;
- reasoning processes;
- synthesis relationships;
- governance decisions;
- action context;
- historical evolution.

---

## Continuity Domains

Includes:

- cognitive history;
- knowledge relationships;
- reasoning lineage;
- synthesis lineage;
- governance continuity;
- adaptive evolution.

## Continuity Requirements

Cognitive continuity must preserve:

- originating states;
- transformation pathways;
- decision context;
- validation history;
- revision records;
- future interpretability.

---

## Principle

Cognitive Continuity Preservation ensures future architectural intelligence remains connected to the cognitive processes that created previous understanding.
