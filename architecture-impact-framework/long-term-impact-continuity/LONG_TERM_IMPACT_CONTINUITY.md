# Long-Term Impact Continuity

Version:

v1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Define the continuity framework for preserving and tracking ORYNTH / Morning Star architectural impact over extended time horizons.

---

## Impact Continuity Principle

Impact is not a single measurement event.

Long-term impact requires preservation of:

- baseline conditions;
- outcome history;
- measurement methods;
- validation records;
- systemic effects;
- future comparisons.

---

## Continuity Domains

Includes:

- historical impact tracking;
- evolving measurement standards;
- outcome persistence;
- ecosystem effects;
- future impact assessment.

---

## Impact Continuity Lifecycle


IMPACT IDENTIFIED

↓

MEASUREMENT ESTABLISHED

↓

OUTCOMES VALIDATED

↓

SYSTEMIC EFFECTS OBSERVED

↓

LONG-TERM CHANGES TRACKED

↓

IMPACT LEGACY PRESERVED


---

## Continuity Requirements

Long-term impact continuity must preserve:

- measurement lineage;
- evidence relationships;
- interpretation context;
- uncertainty records;
- architectural version relationships.

---

## Principle

Long-Term Impact Continuity ensures architectural effects remain understandable and measurable across future states.
